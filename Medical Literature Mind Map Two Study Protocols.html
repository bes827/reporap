Medical Literature Mind Map: Two Study Protocols
  • - This mind map contains structured summaries of two separate medical research proposals.
  • - Article 1: Pharmacist-Led Board Authorized Protocols (BAPs) for Acute Conditions.
  • - Article 2: Regenerative Peripheral Nerve Interface (RPNI) for Postmastectomy Pain Syndrome (PMPS).
Article 1: Pharmacist-Led Board Authorized Protocols (BAPs) for Acute Conditions
  • - A study proposal to compare outcomes for patients receiving care by pharmacists for UTIs, GAS, and COVID-19 to those receiving care from traditional providers.
Knowledge Test
  • - What is the age range for the UTI BAP?
  • - What is the primary antibiotic for the GAS protocol in this study?
  • - Within how many days must nirmatrelvir/ritonavir be initiated for COVID-19?
  • - What is the definition of recurrent UTIs in the exclusion criteria?
  • - What is the comparator effectiveness rate for nirmatrelvir/ritonavir in this study?

Specific Aims & Hypothesis
  • - Objective: To compare outcomes for patients treated by pharmacists under Board Authorized Protocols (BAPs) versus traditional providers for three acute conditions: UTI, GAS, and COVID-19.
Hypothesis
  • - Patients receiving care under pharmacist-driven BAPs will have similar outcomes compared to those receiving care under traditional providers.


Approach & Methodology
  • - The study will utilize a three-manuscript approach, one for each disease state.
Study Design
  • - Design: Retrospective single cohort study.
  • - Rationale: To review the effectiveness of an already implemented clinical practice change.

Study Population
  • - Source: Patients at UK HealthCare at specified locations.
  • - Timeframe: Encounters between October 1, 2022 and September 30, 2025.
  • - Inclusion: Any patient who received testing and treatment for UTI, GAS, or COVID-19 by a pharmacist under a BAP.
  • - A single patient can be included multiple times for multiple encounters.

Data Collection
  • - Source: Electronic Health Record (EHR).
  • - Data collection will involve both easily extractable data and manual chart review.


Disease Protocol 1: Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)
  • - Focuses on pharmacist-managed uncomplicated UTI in women.
Inclusion Criteria
  • - Gender: Female.
  • - Age: 18-64 years old.
  • - History: Prior history of at least one UTI.
  • - Symptoms: At least one symptom (dysuria, frequency, urgency).
  • - Test: Positive Point of Care Test (POCT) for nitrites and leukocytes.

Exclusion Criteria
  • - Male, age <18 or ≥65, pregnant, post-menopausal.
  • - Symptoms of vaginitis or symptoms >1 week.
  • - Immunocompromised status.
  • - Abnormal urinary tract structure.
  • - Concern for pyelonephritis (fever ≥100.4°F, N/V, flank pain).
  • - Diabetes, renal dysfunction.
  • - Recent UTI treatment or inpatient admission (within 1 month).
  • - Recurrent UTIs (defined as four or more a year).

Outcomes & Analysis
  • - Primary Outcome: Rate of clinical cure compared to literature values.
  • - Clinical cure is defined as: no repeat UTI testing/treatment sought within 7 days of the original POCT.
  • - Secondary Outcomes: Time to prescription pick-up, adverse events requiring follow-up within 7 days.
  • - Statistical Test: Student’s t-test for the primary outcome.
Antibiotic-Specific Comparators
  • - Nitrofurantoin: Comparator cure rate of 79-92%.
  • - SMX-TMP: Comparator cure rate of 79-100%.
  • - Fosfomycin: Comparator cure rate of 79-92%.



Disease Protocol 2: Group A Streptococcus (GAS)
  • - Focuses on pharmacist-managed GAS pharyngitis.
Inclusion Criteria
  • - Age: 5 years old or older.
  • - Symptoms: Consistent with GAS (sore throat, fever, swollen lymph nodes, etc.).
  • - Test: Positive GAS POCT.

Exclusion Criteria
  • - Age <5, pregnant/breastfeeding.
  • - Renal dysfunction, immunocompromised.
  • - History of rheumatic fever/heart disease, scarlet fever, or GAS-induced glomerulonephritis.
  • - Antibiotic therapy within 1 month for similar symptoms.
  • - Clinically unstable (e.g., BP <90/60, HR >125, RR >30, Temp ≥103°F).
  • - Concern for viral symptoms (rhinorrhea, cough, oral ulcers).

Outcomes & Analysis
  • - Primary Outcome: Rate of clinical cure compared to literature.
  • - Clinical cure defined as: no repeat testing/treatment sought within 7 days of original POCT.
  • - Study Scope: Only patients receiving amoxicillin will be included.
  • - Secondary Outcomes: Time to prescription pick-up, adverse events within 7 days.
  • - Statistical Test: Student’s t-test.
Comparator Data
  • - Literature focuses on bacteriologic failure, not clinical cure.
  • - A small pediatric study will be used as a comparator.
  • - Comparator value: Treatment failure with persistent symptoms at day 6 is around 4.75%.



Disease Protocol 3: COVID-19 (Nirmatrelvir/Ritonavir)
  • - Focuses on pharmacist-managed dispensing of nirmatrelvir/ritonavir (Paxlovid).
Inclusion Criteria
  • - Age: ≥ 12 years old.
  • - Weight: ≥ 40kg.
  • - Test: Positive SARS-CoV-2 test within 5 days.
  • - Risk: At risk for hospitalization or severe disease progression.
  • - Must have received nirmatrelvir/ritonavir treatment.

Exclusion Criteria
  • - Renal: Severe impairment (eGFR <30ml/min).
  • - Hepatic: Severe impairment (Child-Pugh Class C).
  • - Drug Interactions: Potential for life-threatening reactions or treatment failure.
  • - Requiring hospital admission.

Outcomes & Analysis
  • - Primary Outcome: Hospital admission or death within 30 days of a positive test.
  • - Comparator: An effectiveness of 80% in preventing the primary outcome.
  • - Secondary Outcomes: Time to prescription pick-up, adverse events within 7 days.
  • - Statistical Test: Student’s t-test.
Comparator Rationale
  • - Based on a 2022 US study showing 79.6% effectiveness if given within 5 days.
  • - Effectiveness increased to 89.6% if dispensed on the same day as testing.




Article 2: Regenerative Peripheral Nerve Interface (RPNI) for Postmastectomy Pain Syndrome (PMPS)
  • - A study proposal to prospectively evaluate the efficacy of RPNI in preventing and treating chronic neuropathic pain in breast cancer patients undergoing mastectomy.
Knowledge Test
  • - What percentage of mastectomy patients experience PMPS?
  • - How is PMPS defined in terms of duration?
  • - What is the target sample size per group in this study?
  • - What is the primary pain questionnaire being used?
  • - At what time point is the post-procedure evaluation conducted for both aims?

Background: Postmastectomy Pain Syndrome (PMPS)
  • - Definition: Moderate to severe chronic neuropathic pain that persists for at least six months and is present at least 50% of the time.
  • - Prevalence: Occurs in 35% to 60% of breast cancer patients post-mastectomy.
  • - Origin: Believed to result from surgical injury to major peripheral nerves (e.g., intercostal nerves), leading to neuroma formation.
Clinical Manifestations
  • - Dull dragging sensation, shooting neuropathic pain, or numbness.
  • - Allodynia: Pain from normally non-painful stimuli.
  • - Hyperalgesia: Exaggerated response to painful stimuli.


Intervention: Regenerative Peripheral Nerve Interface (RPNI)
  • - Technique: An established surgical technique to control postamputation pain.
  • - Procedure: A muscle graft (harvested from nearby pectoralis or serratus muscles) is wrapped around the nerve ending and sutured in place.

Specific Aims & Hypotheses
  • - The study has two primary aims: one focused on prevention and the other on treatment.
Aim 1: Prevention
  • - Objective: Determine if RPNI can prevent chronic neuropathic chest pain in patients undergoing mastectomies.
  • - Hypothesis: Patients who undergo RPNI will have no or almost no neuropathic chest pain compared to those who do not.

Aim 2: Treatment
  • - Objective: Determine if RPNI can decrease pain levels in patients with established PMPS.
  • - Hypothesis: Patients with PMPS who undergo RPNI will have a significant decrease in their pain levels.


Approach & Methodology
  • - Study Design: Cohort prospective study.
  • - Enrollment Period: December 1st, 2025 to December 30th, 2030.
Study Population
  • - Recruitment: Breast cancer patients who underwent unilateral or bilateral mastectomies.
Inclusion Criteria
  • - Female, adult patients > 18 years old.
  • - Underwent unilateral or bilateral total skin-sparing mastectomies.
  • - AND either underwent prophylactic RPNI OR have PMPS and are interested in RPNI for treatment.

Exclusion Criteria
  • - Males, pregnant, or prisoners.
  • - Female patients < 18 years old.
  • - Patients who do not consent.

Sample Size
  • - Target: 100 patients per group (Aim 1 and Aim 2).
  • - Power: Designed to have 80% power to detect an effect size of 0.39 in mean pain level.


Experimental Conditions
  • - The study follows two distinct cohorts based on the aims.
Aim 1 Cohort (Prevention)
  • - Recruit 100 patients who underwent mastectomy with RPNI.
  • - Follow-up: Complete a survey and attend a clinical evaluation at 6 months post-surgery.

Aim 2 Cohort (Treatment)
  • - Recruit 100 patients with established PMPS interested in RPNI.
  • - Procedure: Complete a pre-surgical survey and evaluation.
  • - Follow-up: Complete the same survey and evaluation at 6 months post-RPNI surgery.


Data Collection & Endpoints
  • - Data will be collected on demographics, disease characteristics, surgery details, and pain medication use.
Survey-Based Outcomes
  • - Short-form McGill Pain Questionnaire 2 (SF-MPQ-2): Assesses pain quality and intensity (0-10 scale).
  • - Pain Level: Single question on average chest pain in the last week (0-10).
  • - Breast-Q Questionnaire v2: Physical Well-Being: Chest (PWBC) scale for satisfaction (0-100).

Clinical Evaluation Outcomes
  • - Tinel’s Sign: Assessed for presence or absence.
  • - Pressure Algometry: Quantifies mechanical pain threshold at standardized sites.


Bias Management
  • - Selection Bias: Minimized by clear inclusion/exclusion criteria.
  • - Measurement Bias: Minimized by using standardized surveys and tools.
  • - Observer Bias: The researcher performing clinical evaluations will be blinded to the participant's group and evaluation timing.
  • - Reporting Bias: Minimized by a predefined analysis plan.

Data Analysis
  • - Statistical Significance: Set at P < 0.05.
Variable Analysis
  • - Continuous Variables: Expressed as means (SD). Compared using T-test or multiple regression for confounding variables.
  • - Pre/Post Comparison (Aim 2): A paired T-test will be used.
  • - Categorical Variables: Presented as frequencies (%). Compared using chi-square or Fisher's exact tests.
