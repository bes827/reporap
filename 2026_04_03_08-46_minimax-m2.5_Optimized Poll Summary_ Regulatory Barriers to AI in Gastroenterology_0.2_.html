Optimized Poll Summary: Regulatory Barriers to AI in Gastroenterology
  • Source: ASGE AI Summit (Gastroenterologists and GI Professionals)
  • Topic: Regulatory Barriers to Artificial Intelligence in Gastroenterology
  • Author(s): Poll administered at ASGE AI Summit
1. Executive Summary
  • Central finding: REIMBURSEMENT is the overwhelming #1 priority across all questions
  • Secondary priorities: LIABILITY concerns and FDA APPROVAL SPEED
  • Majority expressed skepticism about current regulatory frameworks being adequate for patient safety
  • Demand signals for updated AI-specific guidance
2. Question-by-Question Breakdown
2.1. Q1: Regulatory Adequacy

Question: "Do you believe the current regulatory framework for AI in healthcare is adequate to ensure patient safety?"

  • Response: MAJORITY SAID NO / UNSURE
  • Most respondents expressed skepticism or uncertainty about adequacy
  • Minority felt current system was sufficient
  • Key sentiment: Regulatory landscape perceived as lagging behind AI innovation
2.2. Q2: Roadblocks Legislation Can Solve

Question: "What are the roadblocks which new legislation can solve concerning AI in medicine?"

  • Top Response (ranked by audience vote): REIMBURSEMENT --> most cited
  • Second Response: LIABILITY
  • Key insight: Financial and legal accountability are primary perceived barriers to AI adoption
  • Not purely technical or scientific barriers
  • Reimbursement structures do not yet accommodate AI-assisted clinical decision-making
  • Unclear liability frameworks deter both industry and clinical adoption
2.3. Q3: Regulation to Accelerate Innovation

Question: "Name one regulation/deregulation you think can help accelerate innovation in the GI space?"

  • Top Response (ranked by audience vote): REIMBURSEMENT reform --> consistent with Q2
  • Second Response: FASTER APPROVAL / FDA PROCESS IMPROVEMENTS
  • Key insight: Financial access (reimbursement) is single most impactful lever for accelerating AI innovation in GI
  • Streamlining FDA pathway --> secondary priority (e.g., faster 510(k) or de novo review, predetermined change control plans/PCCPs)
3. Cross-Poll Themes (Synthesis)
3.1. Regulatory Adequacy Gap
  • Clear majority do not trust current regulatory framework to adequately ensure safety
  • Suggests demand for updated, AI-specific guidance
3.2. Reimbursement as Top Barrier
  • Consistently ranked #1 across ALL questions
  • Without payer recognition --> AI devices cannot achieve meaningful clinical penetration regardless of regulatory approval
3.3. Liability Uncertainty
  • Respondents flagged unclear liability assignment (between developer, hospital, and physician)
  • Significant blocker requiring legislative clarification
3.4. Need for Faster FDA Pathways
  • Physicians and industry see value in more agile approval mechanisms
  • Particularly for iterative AI improvements (PCCPs)
  • Rare GI disease applications --> de novo process seen as barrier
