0. ACG Clinical Guideline: Diagnosis & Management of Eosinophilic Esophagitis
1. Study Design & Methodology
  • GRADE methodology applied throughout
  • Systematic literature search (PubMed)
  • PICO framework for all questions
  • Evidence graded: high → very low
1.1 Guideline Development
  • Multidisciplinary panel (adult & pediatric GI, allergist)
  • Regular meetings; consensus voting
1.2 Evidence Review Process
  • Librarian performed exhaustive PubMed searches
  • Cross‑checked with known RCT registries
  • Selected highest‑quality studies per PICO
2. Population Characteristics & Epidemiology
  • Chronic immune‑mediated esophageal disease
  • Symptoms: dysphagia, food impaction, chest pain
  • Age range: infants → adults (all ages)
  • Atopic comorbidities in 60–80% (asthma, eczema, rhinitis)
2.1 Risk Factors
  • Environmental triggers + genetic/epigenetic changes
  • Family history ↑ risk; “tip of iceberg” likely
2.2 Diagnostic Criteria (core triad)
  • Esophageal dysfunction symptoms
  • ≥ 15 eos/hpf on biopsy
  • Exclude other eosinophilic disorders
3. Interventions Overview

All anti‑inflammatory and fibrostenotic therapies are addressed.

3.1 Proton Pump Inhibitors (PPIs)
  • “High‑dose” regimen (≈ 2× reflux dose)
  • Mechanisms: ↓ eotaxin‑3, barrier restoration, IL‑4/13 modulation
  • Conditional recommendation (low evidence)
3.2 Swallowed Topical Steroids (STCs)
  • Budesonide oral suspension (BOS) or orodispersible tablet (BOT)
  • Fluticasone inhaler adapted for esophageal delivery
  • Strong recommendation (moderate evidence)
3.2.1 Dosing Highlights
  • BOS 2 mg BID; BOT 1 mg BID (adults)
  • Pediatric doses weight‑adjusted (0.5–2 mg/day)
3.3 Dietary Elimination Strategies
  • Empiric elimination diets (1‑FED, 2‑FED, 4‑FED, 6‑FED)
  • Elemental formula reserved for infants/severe refractory cases
  • Conditional recommendation (low evidence)
3.3.1 Efficacy Ranges
  • 1‑FED ≈ 35–45% histologic remission
  • 6‑FED ≈ 40–70% remission; most common triggers: milk, wheat
3.4 Biologics
  • Dupilumab (IL‑4Rα antagonist) – approved ≥12 yr, weight ≥ 40 kg
  • Conditional recommendation (moderate evidence)
  • Pediatric dupilumab (1–11 yr, ≥15 kg) – conditional (low evidence)
3.4.1 Trial Outcomes (Adults)
  • Histologic remission: 60% vs 5% placebo (p<0.001)
  • Dysphagia score ↓ ≈ 22 points vs 10 points (p<0.001)
3.5 Esophageal Dilation
  • Adjunct for fibrostenotic strictures causing dysphagia
  • Low‑quality evidence; conditional recommendation
3.5.1 Technique Principles
  • “Start low, go slow” incremental dilators
  • Target lumen ≈ 16 mm (adults) over ≤2 sessions
3.6 Supportive Therapies
  • Feeding therapy for children with aversion/dysfunction
  • Nutritionist involvement for diet adherence & growth monitoring
4. Monitoring, Assessment & Maintenance (first segment)
  • Evaluate symptoms, endoscopy, histology after 8–12 weeks of therapy
  • Use EREFS for endoscopic scoring (0‑9 scale)
  • Histologic target: < 15 eos/hpf (< 60 eos/mm²)
4.1 Response Metrics
  • Clinical improvement ↑ patient‑reported dysphagia scores
  • Endoscopic response: ΔEREFS ≤ 2 points
  • Histologic response: ↓ ≥ 85% eosinophils from baseline
4.2 Maintenance Strategies (initial phase)
  • Continue effective therapy long‑term; disease recurs if stopped
  • Consider dose taper for STCs after remission (BOT 0.5 mg BID possible)
  • PPIs and diet can be maintained at effective doses

The above sections constitute the first portion of the guideline, covering study methodology, patient population, therapeutic options, and initial monitoring principles.

5. Safety & Adverse Events
5.1 Proton Pump Inhibitors
  • Generally well tolerated → low serious AE rate
  • Possible ↑ risk: C. difficile infection (rare)
  • No confirmed CKD, dementia, bone fracture link
5.2 Swallowed Topical Steroids
  • Most common AE: oral/esophageal candidiasis
  • BOS 3.8% → 23% across formulations
  • Adrenal insufficiency:
  • Incidence ~0.5% in induction trials
  • Monitor cortisol if >6 mo therapy
5.2.1 Management of Candidiasis
  • Topical clotrimazole or oral fluconazole course
  • Dose reduction or temporary cessation if severe
5.3 Dietary Elimination
  • Nutritional deficiencies possible → dietitian oversight required
  • Poor adherence ↑ risk of relapse (≈ 50% non‑adherent)
  • Elemental formula: high cost, may need gastrostomy
5.4 Biologics (Dupilumab)
  • Injection site erythema → most frequent AE
  • Rare arthralgia; no increased infection risk reported
  • No routine laboratory monitoring needed
5.5 Esophageal Dilation
  • Perforation rate ≈ 0.05% → rare but serious
  • Post‑procedure hospitalization < 1%
  • “Start low, go slow” reduces complication risk
6. Subgroup & Sensitivity Analyses
6.1 Age‑Based Subgroups
  • Adults ≥12 yr: dupilumab approved; higher response rates
  • Children 1–11 yr: lower histologic remission (68% high dose)
6.1.1 Pediatric Growth Considerations
  • Monitor height, weight, BMI z‑scores every 3 mo
  • STC‑related adrenal suppression rare but assess if >12 mo
6.2 Atopic Burden Subgroups
  • ≥3 concurrent atopic diseases → ↑ EoE suspicion
  • May predict better response to dupilumab (shared pathway)
6.3 Fibrostenotic Phenotype
  • Strictures present → combine anti‑inflammatory + dilation
  • Higher relapse risk if inflammation untreated alone
6.3.1 Endoscopic Findings Correlating with Fibrosis
  • Rings, strictures, reduced distensibility index (< 4.5 mm²)
6.4 Treatment‑Naïve vs. Refractory Subgroups
  • Prior STC failure → consider dupilumab or step‑up diet
  • PPI non‑responders: proceed to STC or biologic
7. Interpretation & Clinical Implications
7.1 Therapeutic Algorithm Summary
  • Initial choice: medication vs diet (shared decision)
  • PPIs or STC first‑line; assess response at 8–12 wk
  • Non‑responders → dupilumab or intensified diet (4‑FED/6‑FED)
7.1.1 Dose Optimization Pathway
  • Successful remission → consider dose taper (STC)
  • Maintain lowest effective dose for maintenance
7.2 Long‑Term Disease Management
  • Chronic condition → indefinite therapy recommended
  • Regular endoscopic surveillance every 12–24 mo if stable
  • Escalate to combination therapy only if persistent fibrostenosis
7.3 Cost & Access Considerations
  • Biologics high cost → insurance prior authorization often needed
  • Generic PPIs inexpensive; preferred for initial trial
  • Dietitian services may be reimbursed under nutrition counseling codes
8. Emerging Research & Future Directions
8.1 Biomarker Development
  • Non‑invasive: Cytosponge, Esophageal String Test (eotaxin‑3)
  • Aim: replace routine biopsies for monitoring
8.2 Novel Therapeutics Pipeline
  • Anti‑TSLP (tezepelumab), anti‑IL‑15, JAK inhibitors in phase II trials
  • New topical steroid delivery systems (nanoparticle gels) under investigation
8.3 Phenotype & Endotype Stratification
  • I‑SEE scoring integration with treatment selection pending validation
  • Genetic/epigenetic markers (e.g., TGF‑β SNPs) explored for response prediction
8.4 Comparative Effectiveness Studies Needed
  • Head‑to‑head RCTs: PPIs vs STC vs diet vs biologic
  • Longitudinal studies on fibrosis progression despite histologic remission
9. Maintenance Therapy
9.1 Rationale for Continuation
  • Disease chronic → relapse if stopped
  • Fibrostenosis progression without ongoing control
9.2 Pharmacologic Maintenance Options
  • Continue effective STC at lowest dose maintaining remission
  • High‑dose PPI maintenance acceptable if responsive
9.2.1 Dose Reduction Strategies
  • BOS 0.5 mg BID after ≥6 mo remission
  • Fluticasone 110 µg BID minimum effective dose
9.3 Dietary Maintenance Options
  • Continue empiric elimination diet long‑term if tolerated
  • Periodic re‑evaluation for trigger identification every 12–24 mo
9.3.1 “Diet Holiday” Concept
  • Temporary relaxation during vacations or special events
  • Bridge with medical therapy to maintain remission
9.4 Biologic Maintenance
  • Dupilumab continued indefinitely; assess response annually
  • Monitor injection site reactions, eosinophil counts periodically
10. Monitoring & Evaluation of Response
10.1 Timing of Assessments
  • Endoscopy + biopsies at 8–12 wk after therapy initiation
  • Repeat monitoring every 12 mo if stable; sooner if symptoms recur
10.2 Clinical Symptom Assessment
  • Dysphagia score (0‑10) → aim ↓≥ 3 points
  • Record IMPACT behaviors (IMBIBE, MODIFY, PROLONG, AVOID, CHEW, TURN)
10.2.1 Symptom Tools
  • Dysphagia Symptom Questionnaire (DSQ) validated in adults
  • Pediatric symptom diaries for feeding dysfunction
10.3 Endoscopic Scoring (EREFS)
  • Edema, Rings, Exudates, Furrows, Strictures → total 0‑9
  • Treatment response threshold: ΔEREFS ≤ 2 points
10.3.1 Fibrostenotic Indicators
  • Persistent rings or strictures → consider dilation adjunct
10.4 Histologic Evaluation
  • Peak eos/hpf < 15 (or < 60 eos/mm²) target
  • Report additional features: basal zone hyperplasia, lamina propria fibrosis (EoEHSS)
10.4.1 Deep Remission Definition
  • Symptom resolution + EREFS 0 + eos/hpf < 5
10.5 Non‑Invasive Monitoring Options
  • Cytosponge sensitivity 75%, specificity 86% vs biopsy
  • Esophageal String Test eotaxin‑3 AUC 0.83 for activity
11. Pediatric‑Specific Considerations
11.1 Growth & Development Goals
  • Maintain height, weight, BMI z‑scores within normal range
  • Monitor every 3 mo; involve pediatric endocrinology if faltering
11.2 Feeding Therapy Recommendations
  • Evaluate for avoidant/restrictive food intake disorder (ARFID)
  • Refer to feeding therapist when dysphagia or aversion present
11.2.1 Multidisciplinary Approach
  • Gastroenterologist ↔ dietitian ↔ feeding therapist ↔ allergist
11.3 Diagnostic Imaging in Children
  • Esophagram recommended for suspected fibrostenosis (very low evidence)
  • EndoFLIP optional where available for distensibility assessment
11.4 Dietary Management in Pediatrics
  • Start with less‑restrictive diet (1‑FED or 2‑FED) preferred
  • Elemental formula reserved for infants or severe refractory cases
11.4.1 Nutritional Support
  • Registered dietitian ensures adequate calories, micronutrients
  • Monitor for vitamin D, iron, zinc deficiencies quarterly
11.5 Medication Dosing in Children
  • PPI: 2 mg/kg/day (max 40 mg BID)
  • BOS: 0.5–1 mg BID based on weight tier
  • Dupilumab: 200 mg q2w for 15‑30 kg; 300 mg weekly ≥ 40 kg
11.6 Adverse Event Monitoring in Children
  • Screen for adrenal insufficiency if STC > 12 mo
  • Watch for growth deceleration with chronic steroid use
12. Key Concepts & Expert Advice
12.1 Diagnostic Nuances
  • Perform endoscopy off any diet/PPI to maximize sensitivity
  • Assess for non‑EoE eosinophilic disorders (GERD, infections)
12.2 Treatment Selection Principles
  • Shared decision‑making essential; consider patient preference, cost, lifestyle
  • No superiority data → choose medication vs diet based on individual factors
12.3 Monitoring Philosophy
  • Do not rely solely on symptoms; combine clinical, endoscopic, histologic data
  • “Deep remission” ideal but achievable in minority of patients
12.4 Long‑Term Disease Outlook
  • EoE does not resolve spontaneously; maintenance required indefinitely
  • Early treatment may limit fibrostenotic progression
12.5 Research Gaps Highlighted
  • Need head‑to‑head RCTs comparing PPIs, STCs, diets, biologics
  • Development of validated non‑invasive biomarkers critical
  • Phenotype/endotype classification to personalize therapy pending validation
