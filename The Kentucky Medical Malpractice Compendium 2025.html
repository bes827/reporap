The Constitutional Landscape
Kentucky's Unique Liability Environment
  • - Kentucky is a "no-cap" state; there are no statutory limits on non-economic or total damages.
  • - Section 54 of the Ky. Constitution: Explicitly forbids the General Assembly from limiting recovery for injury or death.
  • - No Patient Compensation Fund (PCF) exists; financial risk is concentrated on the physician and primary carrier.
Regional Comparison (2025)
  • - Indiana: Total damage cap of $1.8 Million.
  • - Ohio: Non-economic cap of $250k - $350k.
  • - Kentucky: None / Unlimited (See Table 1).


Core Legal Elements
The 2024 ALI Restatement Shift
  • - Plaintiff must prove 4 elements: Duty, Breach, Causation, and Damages.
  • - Standard of Care Shift: Moving from "Customary Practice" (what others do) to "Reasonableness" (competence under circumstances).
  • - Juries can now override medical custom if it contradicts Evidence-Based Medicine (EBM).
Causation Standards
  • - Expert testimony must meet the "Reasonable Medical Probability" standard.
  • - Testimony of "possibility" or speculation is insufficient to survive summary judgment (Adams v. Nasim, M.D.).

Comparative Negligence
  • - Kentucky uses "Pure Comparative Negligence".
  • - A patient can recover damages even if they are 99% at fault.
  • - Award is reduced by the patient's percentage of fault (e.g., non-compliance).


Procedural Barriers & Timelines
  • - Statute of Limitations: Generally one year from discovery of injury (KRS 413.140).
  • - Statute of Repose: Kentucky has no absolute time-bar (5-year limit ruled unconstitutional).
KRS 411.167: Certificate of Merit
  • - Mandatory affidavit certifying a qualified expert has reviewed the case and found merit.
  • - Strict Compliance: Failure to file at the time of the complaint results in dismissal with prejudice (Sanchez v. McMillion).


2025 Regulatory Updates
AI, Telehealth, & Decriminalization
  • - Decriminalization: Kentucky is the first state to shield providers from criminal charges for unintentional medical errors (2024 Law).
  • - AI Mandates (HB 1008): Must disclose to patients if clinical info is generated by Generative AI.
  • - Telehealth: Standard of care is identical to in-person; jurisdiction is usually where the patient is located.

Professional Liability Insurance
  • - Claims-Made: Covers incidents occurring and reported while policy is active.
  • - Occurrence: Covers incidents during the policy period regardless of when filed.
  • - Tail Coverage: Costs 200% to 300% of the last annual premium.
2025 Premium Estimates
  • - OB/GYN: ~$88,000/year.
  • - Internal Medicine/Peds: ~$16,000/year.
  • - "Going Bare": High risk; defense costs alone can reach $100k - $250k.


Malpractice Stress Syndrome (MMSS)
  • - 95% of sued physicians experience MMSS.
  • - Symptoms: Anxiety, "diminished thinking capacity," and Major Depressive Episodes (in 40% of cases).
  • - Marital Impact: High correlation with divorce; only communications with attorney, clergy, or therapist are fully privileged.

Behavioral Dos and Don'ts
  • - DO: Notify carrier immediately; secure the original paper chart; participate in mock depositions.
  • - DON'T: Alter the medical record (metadata will expose it); discuss the case with colleagues; "cyber-stalk" the patient.
The 'Lake Cumberland' Ruling
  • - Kentucky does not recognize the stand-alone tort of "negligent credentialing".
  • - Protects hospitals from suits based solely on granting privileges, absent blatant red flags.
