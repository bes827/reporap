Regenerative Peripheral Nerve Interface: A great option to prevent and decrease pain for patients with post-mastectomy pain syndrome
Outcomes from Kentucky Board of Pharmacy Board Authorized Protocols
  • - Two separate study protocols: (1) prospective cohort protocol evaluating RPNI for prevention/treatment of PMPS; (2) retrospective cohort evaluation of Kentucky Board Authorized Protocols (BAPs) for UTI, GAS, and COVID-19 treatment by pharmacists.
  • - Key study periods: RPNI enrollment planned Dec 1, 2025 - Dec 30, 2030; Kentucky BAP data window Oct 1, 2022 - Sep 30, 2025.
  • - Metadata: single-center (UK/UK Healthcare sites) prospective surgical cohort for RPNI; retrospective single-cohort EHR study across UK Pharmacist Care Clinic for BAPs.

Article 1: RPNI for Post-Mastectomy Pain Syndrome (PMPS)
Prospective surgical cohort protocol
  • - Focus: evaluate RPNI to prevent PMPS and to decrease established PMPS pain levels in mastectomy patients.
  • - Rationale: PMPS is neuropathic, linked to peripheral nerve injury and sensitization; current treatments have inconclusive results.
  • - Primary design: prospective cohort with two aims (prevention arm and treatment arm), follow-up at 6 months.
Introduction & Pathophysiology
  • - PMPS clinical picture: dull dragging, shooting neuropathic pain, numbness -> chest wall, axilla, ipsilateral arm.
  • - Mechanism: peripheral afferent sensitization + central sensitization ---> neuroma formation after sensory nerve injury.
  • - Key statistics: PMPS prevalence reported as 35-60%; defined as pain present ≥ 6 months and ≥ 50% of the time.

Specific Aims & Hypotheses
  • - Aim 1 (prevention): test if intraoperative RPNI at identified nerve endings prevents chronic neuropathic chest pain compared to no RPNI.
  • - Aim 1 hypothesis: patients with intraop RPNI will have no or almost no neuropathic chest pain vs controls.
  • - Aim 2 (treatment): test if RPNI reduces pain in patients with established PMPS when performed postoperatively.

Study population, recruitment & sample size
  • - Target: breast cancer patients post unilateral or bilateral total skin-sparing mastectomy at UK hospitals.
  • - Sample per group: planned 100 patients for prevention arm and 100 patients for treatment arm to detect effect size 0.39 with 80% power.
  • - Recruitment methods: EHR identification, clinic invitations, email, phone calls; informed consent in-person/phone/electronic.

Inclusion & Exclusion criteria
  • - Inclusion: female, age > 18 years, underwent unilateral or bilateral total skin-sparing mastectomy; either prophylactic RPNI or diagnosed PMPS and electing RPNI.
  • - Exclusion: males, pregnancy, prisoners, age < 18 years, lack of consent, failure to meet inclusion.
  • - Note: procedure may be offered as standard of care even if not enrolled in study.

Intervention: Regenerative Peripheral Nerve Interface (RPNI)
  • - Technique: wrap an autologous muscle graft (harvested from nearby pectoralis or serratus) around the transected nerve ending and suture in place.
  • - Purpose: provide a physiological target for regenerating axons to reduce neuroma formation and ectopic firing.
  • - Compared alternative: Targeted Muscle Reinnervation (TMR) also effective but requires nerve target and more microsurgical expertise.

Outcome measures & instruments
  • - Primary PROs: SF-MPQ-2 total and item pain scores; single-item pain level (SF-MPQ-2 Q23) 0-10 scale.
  • - Function/satisfaction: BREAST-Q Physical Well-Being: Chest (PWBC) score (range 0-100; higher = better).
  • - Objective exam (Aim 2): Tinel’s sign and pressure algometry at standardized chest sites to measure mechanical pain threshold (kg or N).

Timing of assessments & follow-up
  • - Aim 1: single survey at 6 months post-mastectomy/RPNI assessing pain and function.
  • - Aim 2: baseline pre-op survey (~2 weeks pre-RPNI) and follow-up at 6 months post-RPNI with blinded clinical exam.
  • - Data recorded with de-identified codes in secured systems.

Data collection variables
  • - Demographics: age, gender, smoking, comorbidities; cancer: stage, reconstruction type.
  • - Surgical details: date of mastectomy, RPNI description/location, nerve(s) treated, time to last follow-up.
  • - Medication and healthcare utilization: pain meds, duration, clinic visits.

Statistical analysis plan
  • - Continuous data: mean (SD); between-group comparisons via t-test; paired pre-post via paired t-test for Aim 2.
  • - Categorical data: frequencies/percentages; chi-square or Fisher's exact test.
  • - Multivariable adjustment: multiple regression to control confounders; significance at P < 0.05.

Bias mitigation & blinding
  • - Use explicit inclusion/exclusion criteria to reduce selection bias.
  • - Standardized surveys and tools to minimize measurement bias.
  • - Clinical evaluator blinded to group and timing to reduce observer bias.
  • - Predefined outcomes and analysis plan to limit reporting bias.

Ethical, safety & retention strategies
  • - IRB review planned; informed consent required; standard clinical safety protocols for surgery.
  • - Retention: reminders (phone/email/MyChart), flexible scheduling, summary of findings, and modest compensation.
  • - Adverse events: documented and managed promptly; procedure available as standard care even if patient declines study participation.

Potential pitfalls & common misunderstandings
  • - PMPS heterogeneity: neuropathic vs nociceptive features may coexist ---> RPNI targets neuropathic neuroma pain, not all chest wall pain.
  • - Expectation bias: surgical patients may report placebo-related improvements; need blinded assessor and objective algometry.
  • - Generalizability: single-center and surgical expertise may limit external validity.

Supporting evidence & prior outcomes
  • - Existing literature: small case series report symptom reduction after intercostal nerve RPNI for PMPS; data limited but positive (see cited case reports).
  • - RPNI established for post-amputation pain control in lower extremity with favorable outcomes.
  • - TMR reported as alternative with success but greater technical demands.


Article 2: Kentucky Board of Pharmacy Board Authorized Protocols (BAPs)
Retrospective cohort evaluation of pharmacist-managed UTI, GAS, COVID-19
  • - Focus: compare clinical outcomes of pharmacist-managed BAP care for UTI, GAS, and COVID-19 to traditional provider outcomes from literature/comparator rates.
  • - Rationale: HRSA-identified primary care shortages; Kentucky expanded pharmacist scope via BAPs to increase access.
  • - Design: retrospective EHR cohort across UK Pharmacist Care Clinic locations; data window Oct 1, 2022 - Sep 30, 2025.
Background & rationale
  • - National gap: 78 million people in primary care HPSAs; > 13,000 providers needed (HRSA, May 2025).
  • - Kentucky: > 1,000 primary care HPSAs; pharmacists can expand access via state BAPs.
  • - Existing evidence: VA pharmacist practitioners manage chronic disease well; acute POCT pharmacist data limited but international studies suggest comparable outcomes and cost benefits.

Overall specific aims & hypotheses
  • - Aim: evaluate whether pharmacist-delivered care under BAPs yields similar clinical outcomes to traditional providers for UTI, GAS, and COVID-19.
  • - Hypothesis: pharmacist-managed BAP care will have outcomes comparable to traditional care for each disease state.
  • - Three-manuscript approach: one manuscript per disease state focusing on disease-specific outcomes.

Study population & design details
  • - Inclusion: patients treated under relevant BAP at UK Healthcare locations during study window; repeat encounters allowed.
  • - No broad exclusion criteria due to retrospective design; each BAP enforces its own eligibility rules.
  • - Data sources: EHR extraction with some manual chart review; no blinding required; expedited IRB likely.

UTI BAP: eligibility & exclusions
  • - Inclusion: female age 18-64 years, prior UTI history, at least one UTI symptom, positive POCT for nitrites+leukocytes.
  • - Major exclusions: male, age ≥ 65, pregnancy, post-menopausal, immunocompromised, structural urinary abnormalities, pyelonephritis signs, recent UTI treatment (within 1 month), recurrent UTIs (≥4/year).
  • - Rationale: select uncomplicated UTI population to match expected cure-rate comparators.

UTI BAP: permitted antibiotics & efficacy comparators
  • - Allowed agents/doses: nitrofurantoin 100 mg PO BID x5d; SMX-TMP 800/160 mg PO BID x3d; fosfomycin 3 g PO once.
  • - Comparator clinical cure rates used for nitrofurantoin: 79-92% (literature-based per agent and population).
  • - Primary outcome: clinical cure = no repeat UTI testing/treatment within 7 days post-index visit (EHR-determined).

UTI BAP: secondary outcomes & analysis
  • - Secondary outcomes: time to prescription pick-up; adverse events requiring follow-up within 7 days.
  • - Statistical test: Student’s t-test for primary comparisons vs literature/comparator rates.
  • - Note: antimicrobial resistance differences require agent-specific comparator benchmarking.

GAS BAP: eligibility & exclusions
  • - Inclusion: age ≥ 5 years (parental consent for minors), symptoms consistent with GAS, positive POCT for GAS.
  • - Exclusions: age <5, pregnancy/breastfeeding, immunocompromised, prior rheumatic fever, recent antibiotics, clinically unstable (severe vitals/AMS), signs suggesting viral illness.
  • - Rationale: ensure appropriate antibiotic prescribing and safety.

GAS BAP: therapy, outcome definitions
  • - Primary agent: amoxicillin (study includes only amoxicillin-treated patients for analysis).
  • - Primary outcome: clinical cure defined as no repeat testing/treatment within 7 days. Comparator failure rate from small pediatric study ~ 4.75% at day 6 used for benchmarking.
  • - Secondary outcomes: time to pick-up and adverse events within 7 days.

COVID-19 BAP (nirmatrelvir/ritonavir) eligibility
  • - Inclusion: age ≥ 12 years, weight ≥ 40 kg, positive SARS-CoV-2 test within 5 days, at risk for progression, ability to assess renal/hepatic function and DDIs.
  • - Exclusions: severe renal impairment (eGFR < 30 ml/min), severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C), major DDIs, currently hospitalized.
  • - Only patients who actually received nirmatrelvir/ritonavir are included in analysis.

COVID-19 BAP: outcomes & comparator effectiveness
  • - Primary outcome: hospitalization or death within 30 days of positive test (EHR-determined).
  • - Literature effectiveness: nirmatrelvir/ritonavir showed estimated effectiveness ~ 79.6% overall; subgroup up to 89.6% if dispensed same day.
  • - For this study, comparator effectiveness chosen as 80% for benchmarking.

Data handling, outcomes definition & analysis
  • - Primary outcomes for each BAP: clinical cure within 7 days for UTI & GAS; 30-day hospitalization/death for COVID-19.
  • - Secondary outcomes: time to pick-up; 7-day adverse events requiring follow-up.
  • - Analysis: Student’s t-test for primary comparisons; descriptive statistics for demographics and visit-level analyses.

Key numeric thresholds & drug regimens (quick reference)
  • - UTI regulated regimens: nitrofurantoin 100 mg PO BID x5d; SMX-TMP 800/160 mg PO BID x3d; fosfomycin 3 g PO once.
  • - GAS: age threshold 5 years; comparator failure rate used 4.75%.
  • - COVID-19: inclusion window 5 days; comparator effectiveness 80% used for study benchmarks.

Limitations, biases & generalizability
  • - Retrospective design -> potential for selection bias, unmeasured confounding, and missing data; some variables need manual chart review.
  • - Comparators often literature-based rather than contemporaneous physician-managed cohorts -> potential temporal or population differences.
  • - BAP implementation heterogeneity across sites may limit external generalizability.

Practical implications & safety considerations
  • - Pharmacist-driven BAPs can increase access in HPSAs but require strict eligibility, DDI screening (esp. nirmatrelvir/ritonavir), and lab capability for renal/hepatic assessment.
  • - Monitoring: ensure mechanism to capture adverse events and timely follow-up; document time-to-dispense to maximize effectiveness (noted benefit if same-day dispensing for COVID-19).
  • - Policy note: BAPs are state-level scope expansions; outcomes data needed to inform broader legislative changes.
