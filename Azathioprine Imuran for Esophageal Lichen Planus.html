Azathioprine (Imuran) for Esophageal Lichen Planus
  • Steroid-sparing immunosuppressant for esophageal lichen planus.
  • Dosing extrapolated from other autoimmune conditions due to lack of disease-specific guidelines.
  • Individualized dose adjustments based on clinical response & tolerability.

Dosing Guidelines
  • No disease-specific dosing guidelines for esophageal lichen planus.
  • Dosing extrapolated from other autoimmune conditions (e.g., rheumatoid arthritis).
  • Individualized dose adjustments based on clinical response & tolerability.
Initial Dosing
  • Typical starting dose: 1–2 mg/kg/day.
Titration & Maximum Dose
  • Titration up to a maximum of 2.5 mg/kg/day.
  • Maximum dose based on use in rheumatoid arthritis & other immunosuppressive indications.
Maintenance Therapy
  • Use the lowest effective dose for maintenance.
Essential Laboratory Monitoring
  • Crucial due to risk of myelosuppression & hepatotoxicity.
  • Regular monitoring ensures patient safety & guides dose adjustments.
Complete Blood Count (CBC) w/ Platelets
  • Monitors for myelosuppression, a significant risk of azathioprine.
Monitoring Frequency
  • Weekly for the first month.
  • Twice monthly for the second & third months.
  • Monthly thereafter, or more frequently if dose changes / other therapy changes occur.
Liver Function Tests (LFTs)
  • Monitors for hepatotoxicity, another key adverse effect.
Monitoring Frequency
  • Every 2 weeks for the first month.
  • Monthly thereafter.
Thiopurine Methyltransferase (TPMT) & NUDT15 Activity
  • Assesses genetic predisposition to altered drug metabolism.
  • Impacts risk of severe myelosuppression.
Recommendation
  • Consider genotyping / phenotyping before initiation.
  • Especially in patients w/ history of myelosuppression or those who develop cytopenias.
Renal Function
  • Monitors kidney health, as renal impairment can affect drug clearance.
Recommendation
  • Monitor periodically.
  • Dose adjustment may be needed in renal impairment.
Important Clinical Considerations
  • Awareness of drug interactions & long-term adverse effects is vital for safe use.
Drug Interactions
  • Specific interactions require dose modification of azathioprine.
Allopurinol Interaction
  • Allopurinol ↑ azathioprine levels → ↑ toxicity risk.
  • Requires azathioprine dose reduction to 1/3–1/4 usual dose.
Adverse Event Monitoring
  • Ongoing vigilance for potential serious complications.
Infection & Malignancy
  • Monitor for signs of infection during therapy.
  • Monitor for signs of malignancy during therapy.
