Islam was the world’s fastest-growing religion from 2010 to 2020
  • Source: Pew Research Center
  • Author: Conrad Hackett
  • Publication Date: 2025 (projected)

Global Religious Population Trends (2010-2020)
  • Analysis of population changes among major religious groups.
  • Data covers 201 countries & territories (99.98% of world pop.).
  • Focus on 7 groups: Christians, Muslims, Hindus, Buddhists, Jews, other religions, & unaffiliated.
Muslim Population: Key Growth Metrics
  • Fastest-growing major religious group from 2010-2020.
  • Total population 2020: 2.0 billion.
  • Absolute growth (2010-2020): +347 million people.
Comparative Growth Analysis
  • Muslim growth (+347M) > all non-Muslim religions combined (+248M).
  • Muslim growth (+347M) > total global Buddhist population in 2020 (324M).
Christian Population: Growth Metrics
  • Remains world's largest religious group.
  • Total population 2020: 2.3 billion.
  • Absolute growth (2010-2020): +122 million.
Growth Limiting Factors
  • Growth rate slowed by high rates of disaffiliation.
Buddhist Population: Negative Growth
  • Only major group w/ ↓ in absolute numbers.
  • Total population 2020: 324 million.
  • Absolute change (2010-2020): -19 million people.
Global Population Share: Shifting Proportions
  • The demographic gap between Muslims & Christians is shrinking.
  • Muslims: 23.9% (2010) → 25.6% (2020).
  • Christians: 30.6% (2010) → 28.8% (2020).
Etiology of Growth: Demographic Factors
  • Rapid growth primarily driven by demographic factors, not religious conversion.
  • Key mechanisms: ↑ fertility rates & ↓ median age.
Fertility Rate Differentials (2015-2020)
  • Muslim women (avg): 2.9 children/lifetime.
  • Non-Muslim women (avg): 2.2 children/lifetime.
Age Demographics: Youth Profile (2020)
  • Global median age (Muslims): 24 years.
  • Global median age (Non-Muslims): 33 years.
  • ∴ 9-year age gap → significant demographic momentum.
Conversion Dynamics: Net Neutral Effect
  • Religious switching has minimal impact on global population change.
  • Estimated loss from Muslim upbringing: ≈1%.
  • This loss is offset by a comparable influx of converts to Islam.
Geographic Distribution of Muslims (2020)
  • Analysis of population distribution by region and country.
  • Highlights concentration outside the traditionally associated Middle East region.
Regional Population Analysis
  • Muslims expanded as a share of the population in most regions from 2010-2020.
  • Top 3 regions by absolute Muslim population size.
1. Asia-Pacific
  • Largest regional Muslim population.
  • Total: 1.2 billion.
2. Middle East-North Africa (MENA)
  • Second-largest regional population: 414 million.
  • Highest concentration: 94% of the regional population is Muslim.
  • Represents only ≈20% of the world's total Muslim population.
3. Sub-Saharan Africa
  • Third-largest regional population.
  • Total: 369 million.
Europe & North America
  • Relatively smaller Muslim populations.
  • Growth rate (2010-2020) was much faster than non-Muslim populations in these regions.
National Population Analysis
  • The countries with the largest Muslim populations are all outside the MENA region.
  • Muslims constitute a majority of the population in 53 countries and territories worldwide.
Top 4 Countries by Muslim Population (2020)
  • 1. Indonesia: 239 million
  • 2. Pakistan: 227 million
  • 3. India: 213 million
  • 4. Bangladesh: 151 million
