üìå Introduction & Key Concepts
  • - Primary prevention: Lowering low-density lipoprotein cholesterol (LDL-C) to reduce atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (CVD) risk in people without established CVD.
  • - CVD Definition: Includes fatal/nonfatal MI, ACS, sudden cardiac death, coronary revascularization, stroke, and peripheral arterial disease.
  • - Core Principle: The decision to use pharmacotherapy is based on both the LDL-C level and the patient's estimated 10-year CVD risk.

üìä CVD Risk Assessment
  • - Perform risk evaluation for patients aged 20 years or at their first encounter if older.
  • - Initial Steps:
  • - Measure a lipid panel: total cholesterol, HDL-C, triglycerides, and LDL-C.
  • - Use an atherosclerotic CVD (ASCVD) risk calculator to estimate 10-year risk for patients aged 30 to 79.
Risk Categories (Author's Preferred)
  • - Low: <5%
  • - Intermediate: 5 to 7.4%
  • - High: 7.5 to 19.9%
  • - Very high: ‚â•20%

  • - The ACC/AHA labels these categories differently:
  • - Intermediate (5-7.4%) is called ‚Äúborderline‚Äù risk.
  • - High (7.5-20%) is called ‚Äúintermediate‚Äù risk.
  • - Very high (‚â•20%) is called ‚Äúhigh‚Äù risk.


Risk-Benefit Discussion
  • - A reasonable expectation for statin therapy is a 30% relative risk reduction.
  • - This translates into an absolute risk reduction that is proportional to the patient's baseline risk.
  • - Example: A 60-year-old man with a 9% 10-year risk would see his risk drop to 6% (a 3 percentage-point absolute reduction).


ü•ó Lifestyle Modification
  • - Recommended for all patients with high LDL-C.
  • - Key Components: Aerobic exercise, healthy diet, and weight loss for those with overweight/obesity.
  • - Obtain a dietary history to identify patterns that raise LDL-C (e.g., ketogenic or paleolithic diets).
Dietary Impact
  • - Dietary modification alone has not shown material decreases in cardiovascular events in prior trials.
  • - Diets shown to lower LDL-C and/or 10-year risk include: Mediterranean, DASH, vegetarian, and vegan.
  • - The Mediterranean diet's CVD benefit may be mediated by lower blood pressure and improved dysglycemia, not primarily lower LDL-C.

Combined Approach
  • - Concomitant exercise and dietary modification lowers LDL-C more effectively than either alone.


üíä Indications for Statin Therapy
  • - Recommendations are based on LDL-C level and baseline CVD risk (see Algorithm 1).
  • - Goal: Reduce CVD events via LDL-C lowering.
  • - Shared decision-making is crucial, especially for intermediate-risk patients.
LDL-C ‚â• 190 mg/dL (‚â•4.9 mmol/L)
  • - Action: Initiate high-intensity statin therapy.
  • - A CVD risk calculation is not needed to start treatment.
  • - Always perform a work-up for Familial Hypercholesterolemia (FH).
High-Intensity Statin Examples (Table 1)
  • - Atorvastatin: 40 to 80 mg daily
  • - Rosuvastatin: 20 to 40 mg daily


LDL-C < 190 mg/dL (<4.9 mmol/L)
  • - For patients without diabetes, statin indication is guided by the 10-year estimated CVD risk group.
High (7.5-20%) & Very High (‚â•20%) Risk
  • - Indication: Initiate statin therapy if LDL-C is >100 mg/dL.
  • - Initial Dosing: Usually start with a moderate-intensity statin.
  • - It is reasonable to start with high-intensity therapy for those with a 10-year risk ‚â•20%.
Moderate-Intensity Statin Examples (Table 1)
  • - Atorvastatin: 10 to 20 mg
  • - Rosuvastatin: 5 to 10 mg

Supporting Evidence (CTT Collaboration)
  • - Meta-analysis of >175,000 patients showed significant risk reduction per 1 mmol/L LDL-C reduction:
  • - Major coronary event: RR 0.79
  • - Stroke: RR 0.85
  • - Coronary revascularization: RR 0.76


Intermediate (5-7.4%) Risk
  • - Action: Undertake shared decision-making.
  • - The absolute benefits are smaller in this group.
  • - CTT meta-analysis showed a modest absolute risk reduction of 0.47% over a five-year period.
Factors Favoring Statin Therapy
  • - LDL-C Level: Recommend statin therapy if LDL-C is >160 mg/dL.
  • - Risk-Enhancing Factors: Presence of these factors favors statin initiation (See Table 2).
Key Risk-Enhancing Factors (Table 2)
  • - Family history of premature ASCVD.
  • - Chronic kidney disease (eGFR 15-59).
  • - Chronic inflammatory conditions (e.g., psoriasis, RA, HIV).
  • - History of preeclampsia.
  • - High-risk ethnicity (e.g., South Asian).

Additional Risk Stratification
  • - Coronary Artery Calcium (CAC): Consider in reluctant patients with LDL-C <160 mg/dL.
  • - Recommend statin if CAC score is ‚â•100 Agatston units (many cardiologists treat for any CAC >0).
  • - If CAC score is 0, statin therapy can be deferred.
  • - Other Markers: Elevated hsCRP (‚â•2 mg/L) or Lp(a) (‚â•50 mg/dL).



Low (<5%) Risk
  • - Action: For most patients, do not start statin therapy.
  • - Absolute risk reduction is very modest (0.18% over five years in CTT meta-analysis).
  • - Calcium scoring can help identify higher-risk individuals within this group.



üîÑ Subsequent Management
  • - Follow-up is crucial to assess efficacy, adherence, and side effects.
Monitoring & Assessment
  • - Repeat LDL-C: Measure 4 to 6 weeks after initiating statin therapy.
  • - Some physicians also check creatinine phosphokinase (CPK) and liver function tests (LFTs).
  • - For patients not starting a statin, reassess ASCVD risk periodically.

Medication Adjustment
  • - Target LDL-C: For primary prevention, a general target is <100 mg/dL.
  • - Action if not at target (in high/very high risk):
  • - 1. Intensify statin dose (e.g., to high-intensity).
  • - 2. Add a non-statin medication.

Side Effects & Adherence
  • - Most major adverse reaction: Muscle symptoms.
  • - Statins can increase risk of new-onset diabetes in a dose-dependent fashion.
  • - Cognitive function: Large trials show no effect.
  • - Assess adherence at follow-up visits; check prescription fill history in EHR.
Managing Statin Intolerance
  • - Consider alternative statins like fluvastatin or pitavastatin.
  • - Use alternate-day regimens (e.g., rosuvastatin 2.5 mg three times per week).



‚ûï Non-Statin Treatment
  • - Used for statin-intolerant patients or as add-on therapy in high-risk patients not at LDL-C goal.
Indications
  • - Statin Intolerance: Ezetimibe and bempedoic acid have shown to reduce CV events in this population.
  • - Inadequate LDL-C Lowering: For high/very high-risk patients not at goal on max-tolerated statin, add ezetimibe 10 mg daily first.

Available Agents (Table 3)
Ezetimibe (First-line non-statin)
  • - LDL Reduction: ~20 to 25%.
  • - Side Effects: No effect on muscle symptoms or diabetes risk.
  • - Evidence: EWTOPIA 75 trial showed benefit in patients ‚â•75 years old.

Bempedoic Acid
  • - LDL Reduction: ~20 to 25%.
  • - Evidence: CLEAR OUTCOMES trial showed reduced CV events in statin-intolerant patients (including primary prevention subgroup).
  • - Side Effects: Higher rates of gout, cholelithiasis, and kidney function impairment.

Colesevelam (Bile Acid Sequestrant)
  • - LDL Reduction: ~15 to 20%.
  • - Barrier to use: Requires six tablets daily.
  • - Lacks an outcomes trial.

PCSK9 Inhibitors
  • - Inclisiran is now FDA-approved for primary prevention.
  • - Barrier to use: Much higher cost.



üë• Special Populations
  • - Management may need to be tailored for specific patient groups.
Age >75 years
  • - Use the same criteria to initiate statin therapy.
  • - Key Point: Relative risk reduction may be smaller, but absolute risk reduction is larger due to high baseline risk.
  • - A meta-analysis found statins reduced major vascular events by 26% per 1 mmol/L LDL-C reduction in this age group.

Diabetes Mellitus
  • - LDL-C ‚â•190 mg/dL: Treat with high-intensity statin (same as non-diabetics).
  • - Age ‚â•40 & LDL-C <190: Start a moderate-intensity statin.
  • - Age 20-39 with risk factors: Shared decision-making about starting a moderate-intensity statin.
  • - High ASCVD risk (‚â•20%): Consider starting a high-intensity statin with a lower LDL-C goal of <70 mg/dL.

Childbearing Potential & Pregnancy
  • - Contraindication: Statins are contraindicated during pregnancy.
  • - Counseling: Discuss the need for effective contraception when initiating a statin.
  • - If trying to conceive: Withdraw the statin.
  • - Reassurance: FDA update found no increased risk if a patient was on a statin when pregnancy was discovered, as long as it is stopped promptly.

üìú Summary & Recommendations
  • - Approach is guided by LDL-C level and 10-year CVD risk.
LDL-C ‚â•190 mg/dL
  • - Recommendation: Treat with high-dose statin therapy (Grade 1A).

LDL-C 100 to <190 mg/dL
  • - High/Very High Risk (>7.5%): Recommend statin therapy (Grade 1A). Start with moderate-intensity.
  • - Intermediate Risk (5-7.4%): Suggest statin therapy (Grade 2A) via shared decision-making. More likely to encourage if LDL-C >160 mg/dL or risk enhancers are present.
  • - Low Risk (<5%): Do not start statin therapy.

Follow-up & Goals
  • - Goal LDL-C: <100 mg/dL.
  • - If goal is not met in high-risk patients, intensify statin and/or add non-statin therapy (ezetimibe first).

Statin-Intolerant Patients
  • - Alternatives: Ezetimibe and bempedoic acid are reasonable alternatives. Ezetimibe is preferred due to its side effect profile.
