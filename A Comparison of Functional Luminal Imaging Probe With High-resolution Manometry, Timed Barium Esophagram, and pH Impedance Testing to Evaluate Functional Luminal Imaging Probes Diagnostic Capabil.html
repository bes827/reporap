A Comparison of Functional Luminal Imaging Probe With High-resolution Manometry, Timed Barium Esophagram, and pH Impedance Testing to Evaluate Functional Luminal Imaging Probe’s Diagnostic Capabilities
  • Journal: Journal of Clinical Gastroenterology
  • Publication Date: November/December 2024
  • Volume/Issue: 58(10):p 981-988
  • DOI: 10.1097/MCG.0000000000001966
Knowledge Test
  • 1. What was the overall diagnostic agreement percentage between FLIP and HRIM for correlated diagnoses in this study?
  • 2. Did the presence of a hiatal hernia significantly impact the agreement between FLIP and TBE normality determinations?
  • 3. Which FLIP balloon fill volume (40 mL, 50 mL, or 60 mL) showed a significantly lower detection rate for active secondary peristalsis?
  • 4. Was FLIP Distensibility Index (DI) found to be correlated with acid exposure time (AET) on pH monitoring?
  • 5. For which specific HRIM diagnosis did FLIP show the highest agreement (78.9%) with its corresponding FLIP diagnosis?
Study Objective & Background
  • Objective: Determine Functional Luminal Imaging Probe's (FLIP) diagnostic utility.
  • Method: Compare FLIP measurements w/ established esophageal evaluation standards.
  • Standards: High-resolution manometry (HRIM), timed barium esophagram (TBE), 24-hour impedance-pH monitoring.
Background on FLIP
  • FLIP: Esophageal evaluation technique performed @ endoscopy.
  • Recent research: Significance in evaluating treatment efficacy of LES interventions, achalasia, eosinophilic esophagitis.
  • Challenge: Variable diagnostic criteria & data collection in current literature → need for standardization.
Background on Established Tests
  • HRIM, pH monitoring, TBE: Long utilized for esophageal function, acid exposure, & stasis.
  • EGJOO: Manometric diagnosis refined in Chicago Classification v4; FLIP data required as confirmatory test.
  • Hiatal Hernia: Known to affect other diagnostic standards; impact on FLIP accuracy needs study.
Methods: Patient Cohort & Test Protocols
  • Retrospective analysis: Single academic center, 413 preintervention patients.
  • Inclusion: FLIP + supporting esophageal studies (pH impedance, TBE, HRIM) w/in 1 year of FLIP.
  • Exclusion: Anorectal/pyloric FLIP, invasive esophageal surgery/procedures (e.g., fundoplication).
Patient Demographics
  • Total patients: 413.
  • Average age: 60.98 years (SD ±15.0), range 20-90 years.
  • Gender: 51% females.
  • Hiatal hernia/esophagitis prevalence: Similar to general population.
High-Resolution Manometry (HRIM) Protocol
  • System: ManoScan ESO High-Resolution Manometry Catheters.
  • Software: ManoView w/ high-resolution esophageal color topography.
  • Preparation: ≥6 hours fasting.
  • Catheter positioning: Hypopharynx to stomach.
  • Protocol: Chicago Classification v3.0/4.0.
  • Data collected: Integrated relaxation pressure (IRP), intrabolus pressure, distal contractile integral (DCI), LES basal sphincter pressure, distal latency, peristalsis presence.
  • Abnormal studies: Evaluated per Chicago Classification v3.0/4.0 criteria.
pH Impedance Monitoring Protocol
  • Catheter: Versaflex-Z pH impedance (Model No. 900003).
  • Sensors: 8 impedance sensors, 4 cm separation.
  • Positioning: Distal pH sensor 5 cm above LES.
  • Monitoring: 24 hours, on/off acid suppression therapy.
  • Parameters recorded: Normal/abnormal study, acid suppression therapy (PPI), total reflux events, total proximal episodes, upright/supine proximal episodes (acidic, weakly acidic, nonacidic), upright/recumbent/total AET, symptom index (SI), symptom association probability (SAP).
pH Impedance Normality Criteria
  • Pathologic total AET: >6.0% (Lyon Consensus).
  • Normal total AET: <4% (Lyon Consensus).
  • Indeterminate total AET: 4% to 6% (Lyon Consensus).
  • Symptom Index (SI): Significant if symptoms present in >50% events.
  • Symptom Association Probability (SAP): Significant @ >95%.
Timed Barium Esophagram (TBE) Protocol
  • Barium administration: 240 mL low-density barium, patient standing.
  • Imaging: Two-on-one spot films @ 1 min & 5 min.
  • Abnormal criterion: Barium column >2 cm @ 5 minutes.
  • Tablet test: Esophagus cleared w/ water, 13 mm barium tablet administered.
  • Tablet passage: Evaluated @ 5 minutes; abnormal if tablet not passed.
Functional Lumen Imaging Probe (FLIP) Protocol
  • Catheter: 16 cm (EndoFLIP EF-322N; Medtronic Inc.), inserted orally during endoscopy.
  • Positioning: LES 'waist' identification on real-time 3D modeling after low-volume inflation (20-30 mL).
  • Measurements: 16 cross-sectional areas, 1 intraballoon pressure @ 10 Hz sampling rate.
  • Distension: Volume ↑ by 10 mL increments, stepwise up to 70 mL (or 60 mL initially).
  • Data recording: Endoscopists waited for esophageal contraction termination & stable readings for 60 seconds.
  • Parameters: DI, diameter, pressure, CSA, compliance (real-time single-point recordings).
  • Diagnoses: Based on peristalsis & esophageal opening classifications (Medtronic White Paper, Carlson et al.).
  • Updated criteria: Diagnoses updated w/ accepted criteria for diameter & DI (post-2021 criteria applied retrospectively).
FLIP Contractile & Opening Classifications
  • Contractile Response: Normal (NCR), Impaired/Disordered (IDCR), Spastic-Reactive (SRCR), Absent (ACR), Borderline (BCR).
  • Esophageal Opening: Reduced (REO), Borderline REO, Borderline Normal, Normal.
FLIP DI Normality Cutoffs
  • Variability in literature: Both 2.0 mm2/mm Hg & 2.8 mm2/mm Hg investigated in analyses.
Data Analysis Methods
  • Statistical analysis: Conducted by institution’s Department of Biostatistics and Data Science.
  • Significance: P value <0.05 considered statistically significant.
  • Tests: Fisher exact analysis, ANOVA, t-test, Cohen kappa test, McNemar tests, Receiver operating characteristic curve.
FLIP vs. HRIM Analysis
  • IRP & DI relationship: Patients grouped by normal IRP (<15 mmHg) + NCR/BCR vs. abnormal IRP (≥15 mmHg) + IDCR/ACR/SRCR.
  • Hiatal hernia impact: Analysis repeated w/ patients divided by hiatal hernia presence (>2 cm, clinically significant).
  • Correlation: Fisher exact analysis for IRP normality & DI normality (cutoffs 2.0 mm2/mm Hg & 2.8 mm2/mm Hg) w/ correlating peristalsis findings.
  • Diagnostic correlation: Physiologically similar diagnoses from FLIP & HRIM paired based on Chicago Classification v4.0 & FLIP criteria.
FLIP vs. pH Impedance Analysis
  • AET normality & DI/contractile pattern: Patients classified by acid suppression status (off/medicated) & AET (normal <4%, indeterminate 4-6%, abnormal >6%).
  • Mean DI: Calculated for each of 6 groups.
  • Statistical tests: ANOVA & t-test to determine if mean DI varied by AET classification.
  • Average AET comparison: Patients grouped by normal DI (≥2.0 mm2/mm Hg) + NCR/BCR vs. abnormal DI (≤2.0 mm2/mm Hg) + IDCR/ACR/SRCR; average AET compared via t-testing.
FLIP vs. TBE Analysis
  • Sensitivity & Specificity: Calculated for FLIP diagnoses & isolated DI cutoffs (<2.0 mm2/mm Hg, <2.8 mm2/mm Hg) vs. TBE as standard.
  • TBE normality cutoff: Column height >2 cm @ 5 minutes.
  • Agreement: Cohen kappa test.
  • Diagnostic variability: McNemar tests for binary variables indicating normality & peristalsis detection.
  • Hiatal hernia impact: Analysis repeated w/ patients divided by hiatal hernia presence.
  • Optimal DI cutoffs: Receiver operating characteristic curve created using DI values vs. TBE normality.
Results: Patient Cohort & Test Utilization
  • Total FLIP patients evaluated: 413.
  • Patients undergoing HRIM: 276.
  • Patients undergoing TBE: 240.
  • Patients undergoing pH monitoring: 164.
Patients Included in Specific Analyses
  • HRIM analysis (IRP/peristalsis consistent): 136 patients.
  • pH impedance analysis (DI/peristalsis consistent): 89 patients.
  • All 136 HRIM analysis patients had hiatal hernia determined by EGD (>2 cm, clinically significant).
Results: High-Resolution Manometry (HRIM) Comparison
  • Abnormal Distensibility Index (DI) → associated w/ abnormal Integrated Relaxation Pressure (IRP) (P = 0.003).
  • Mean DI: Normal IRP (<15 mmHg) + NCR/BCR = 3.57 ± 1.52 mm2/mm Hg.
  • Mean DI: Abnormal IRP (≥15 mmHg) = 2.37 ± 2.04 mm2/mm Hg.
Impact of Hiatal Hernia on DI & IRP Correlation
  • No hernia group:
  • IRP <15 mmHg: Average DI = 3.84 mm2/mm Hg.
  • IRP ≥15 mmHg: Average DI = 2.08 mm2/mm Hg (P = 0.001).
  • Hernia group:
  • IRP <15 mmHg: Average DI = 3.25 mm2/mm Hg.
  • IRP ≥15 mmHg: Average DI = 3.09 mm2/mm Hg (P = 0.793).
  • Significant difference: Among patients w/ IRP ≥15 mmHg, average DI differed by 0.86 mm2/mm Hg between hernia & non-hernia groups (P = 0.025).
Correlation of IRP & DI Normality
  • Fisher exact test: Correlation coefficient <0.0001 for IRP normality & DI normality (cutoff 2.0 mm2/mm Hg) grouped w/ correlating peristalsis findings.
  • Subgroup counts:
  • IRP <15 mmHg: 4 patients w/ DI ≤2.0 mm2/mm Hg; 28 patients w/ DI >2.0 mm2/mm Hg.
  • IRP ≥15 mmHg: 58 patients w/ DI ≤2.0 mm2/mm Hg; 44 patients w/ DI >2.0 mm2/mm Hg.
Agreement Between HRIM & FLIP Diagnoses
  • Overall agreement: 33.5% among analyzed correlated diagnoses.
  • Highest agreement: 78.9% for Type I achalasia (HRIM) & REO w/ ACR (FLIP).
  • Other agreements:
  • Normal (HRIM) & NEO w/ NCR (FLIP): 41.6%.
  • Type III achalasia (HRIM) & REO w/ SRCR (FLIP): 23.8%.
  • EGJOO (HRIM) & BrEO or REO w/ NCR (FLIP): 31.4%.
  • Aperistalsis (HRIM) & NEO w/ ACR (FLIP): 55.5%.
Results: pH Impedance Monitoring Comparison
  • DI not associated w/ Acid Exposure Time (AET).
  • Off-acid suppression patients:
  • DI >2.0 mm2/mm Hg + NCR/BCR: Average AET = 8.81%.
  • DI ≤2.0 mm2/mm Hg + IDCR/ACR/SRCR: Average AET = 11.95% (P = 0.425).
  • Medicated patients:
  • DI >2.0 mm2/mm Hg + NCR/BCR: Average AET = 8.94%.
  • DI ≤2.0 mm2/mm Hg + IDCR/ACR/SRCR: Average AET = 9.0% (P = 0.989).
Average DI by Total AET & Acid Suppression Status
  • Off acid suppression:
  • AET <4%: Mean DI = 3.3 (SD 3.51).
  • AET 4-6%: Mean DI = 2.84 (SD 1.70).
  • AET >6%: Mean DI = 3.4 (SD 2.58).
  • Medicated:
  • AET <4%: Mean DI = 3.5 (SD 1.63).
  • AET 4-6%: Mean DI = 3.1 (SD 1.99).
  • AET >6%: Mean DI = 3.9 (SD 1.95).
Results: Timed Barium Esophagram (TBE) Comparison
  • FLIP diagnoses vs. TBE normality: 50.4% agreement (k = 0.18).
  • Sensitivity for LES pathologies (w/ REO): 97.9%.
  • Specificity for LES pathologies: 38.2%.
DI Cutoffs for Abnormality vs. TBE
  • DI ≤2.0 mm2/mm Hg:
  • Sensitivity: 45.1%.
  • Specificity: 61.9%.
  • Agreement: 59.7% (k = 0.038).
  • DI ≤2.8 mm2/mm Hg:
  • Sensitivity: 60.4%.
  • Specificity: 65.8%.
  • Agreement: 54.6% (k = 0.087).
Impact of Hiatal Hernia on FLIP & TBE Agreement
  • Agreement between FLIP & TBE normality: 50% for both hernia & non-hernia groups.
  • Kappa values: k = 0.18 (hernia), k = 0.20 (no hernia).
  • Conclusion: Hiatal hernia did not significantly affect agreement.
Results: Peristalsis Detection
  • 60 mL fill: Significantly ↓ detection rate of active secondary peristalsis vs. 40 mL (P = 0.0423) & 50 mL (P = 0.0485).
  • No significant difference in detection between 40 mL & 50 mL balloon fills (P = 0.67).
Average Pressure & Peristalsis Detection
  • Average pressure significantly ↑ among those w/ detected peristalsis vs. undetected @ same fill volumes:
  • 40 mL: Detected 24.7 ± 9.6 mmHg vs. Undetected 21.1 ± 11.5 mmHg (P = 0.002).
  • 50 mL: Detected 32.8 ± 12.0 mmHg vs. Undetected 26.5 ± 11.2 mmHg (P < 0.001).
  • 60 mL: Detected 41.8 ± 13.69 mmHg vs. Undetected 36.6 ± 13.0 mmHg (P < 0.001).
  • Clinical relevance: Not a consistent trend indicating improved/diminished induction; no consistent finding of ↑ pressure improving detection w/in same patient.
Discussion & Clinical Implications
  • FLIP as adjunct to HRIM: Supported by strong metric correlation, but imperfect congruence → use in adjunct.
  • FLIP as confirmatory test for EGJOO: Supported by improved accuracy in hiatal hernia patients.
  • FLIP not useful for reflux assessment: No correlation w/ pH impedance findings.
  • Diagnostic agreement between FLIP & TBE: Lower than previous studies (50.4% vs. 78%).
  • Peristalsis pattern variability: Analyze data from each FLIP fill volume for optimal detection.
FLIP & HRIM Relationship
  • Correlation: Distensibility & contractility are correlated markers of esophageal function.
  • Imperfect congruence: Suggests FLIP & HRIM are useful in adjunct to detect LES pathology.
  • Support for Chicago Classification 4.0: Recommends FLIP as adjunct to HRIM.
  • Achalasia/REO: DI is useful adjunct to manometry for LES physiology.
  • Low diagnostic agreement: Overall 33.5% (max 77.8% for Type I achalasia) → FLIP does not approach diagnostic power of manometry alone.
Hiatal Hernia Impact on FLIP & HRIM
  • Previous finding: IRP can be artificially ↑ in hiatal hernia.
  • Study finding: Among patients w/ ↑ IRP, average DI was significantly higher & w/in normal range in hiatal hernia patients vs. w/o.
  • Implication: DI may not be impacted by hiatal hernia as IRP is.
  • Recommendation: Normal range DI + ↑ IRP provides beneficial clinical data to guide management decisions for manometric diagnosis of REO.
FLIP & GERD Assessment
  • AET: No statistical or numeric relation to DI.
  • Abnormal DI: Predicted neither abnormal AET nor abnormal barium retention w/ strong specificity.
  • Conclusion: FLIP not expected to be adequate test for characterizing/quantifying GERD; ↑ DI does not indicate ↑ AET.
FLIP & TBE Agreement Discrepancy
  • Current study agreement: 50.4% (using full diagnostic criteria for EGJOO/pathologies).
  • Previous studies: Found 78% agreement (using only maximum DI as diagnostic criteria).
  • Possible reasons for discrepancy: Lower power of current study (N=242 vs. N=329), less specific criteria in previous studies.
Optimal FLIP Measurement Method
  • Previous thought: 60 mL fill volume w/ 16 cm FLIP catheter most sensitive for esophageal body contractile response.
  • Study finding: 60-70 mL fills were less sensitive & had significantly ↓ overall detection of induced secondary peristalsis vs. 40 mL & 50 mL fills.
  • Implication: Ideal balloon fill volume for detecting contractile response & measuring accurate DI values is not larger fill volumes.
  • Recommendation: Aggregate measurements @ multiple inflations necessary for accurate assessment; single measurement may not consistently fit pathologic changes.
Role of FLIP in Clinical Practice
  • Recommendation: Utilize FLIP in diagnosis of achalasia or EGJOO as supportive study to manometry.
  • Not supported: Utilization of FLIP in evaluation of GERD.
  • Potential uses: Moderate sensitivity makes it fit for detecting structural & motility abnormality, e.g., intraoperative testing.
  • Future research: Validate current standard (8 cm FLIP balloon @ 40 mL fill during fundoplication/myotomy) as optimal protocol.
Limitations of the Study
  • Retrospective, single-center study.
  • Sample sizes for additional testing methods (HRIM, TBE, pH monitoring) were not equal to FLIP cohort.
  • Data collected from procedural reports largely before updated FLIP peristaltic terminology (though cases re-classified).
  • Dry catheter artifact not accounted for (but data not recorded during esophageal contraction).
  • FLIP testing not performed @ time of index endoscopy → preliminary HRIM/EGD diagnoses could be known before FLIP testing.
  • Potential impact: Endoscopist's interpretation of FLIP data could be influenced (though FLIP diagnoses confirmed by procedure report data).
Strengths of the Study
  • Second-largest population in literature for this type of analysis.
  • First study to examine TBE, pH monitoring, & HRIM in relation to FLIP.
Conclusion & Recommendations
  • FLIP as adjunct to HRIM: Supported by strong correlation in measurements, but low diagnostic agreement.
  • FLIP as confirmatory test to HRIM: Supported by improved accuracy in hiatal hernia patients.
  • FLIP for reflux assessment: Not useful; DI not highly correlated to pH monitoring AET.
  • FLIP vs. TBE: Diagnostic agreement 59.7%, lower than previous studies, w/ moderate sensitivity.
  • Peristalsis detection: Significant variability between fill volumes → analyze data from each fill volume for optimal detection.
