Enhancing Gastroenterology Education Through E-Learning
  • Source: Gastrointestinal Endoscopy (ASGE Article), Published online 3 February 2026
  • Topic: E-Learning in Gastroenterology Education
  • Author(s): Saurabh Chawla et al.
1. Introduction and Background
Evolution of E-Learning in Medical Education
  • Traditional CME relies on publications and periodic in-person meetings with didactic lectures or hands-on training delivered to classrooms
  • Textbooks now largely used for acquiring core knowledge or comprehensive review due to rapid advancements
  • Internet enabled widespread, instantaneous dissemination of information → started e-learning era
  • First few decades: e-learning existed as websites housing electronic versions of published documents; web-based search engines made these easily searchable
  • Example: ASGE guidelines page on website → immensely popular with society members globally
E-Learning Acceleration During COVID-19
  • Structured e-learning initially explored as secondary tool for distant learners lacking local expertise or ability to travel to traditional forums
  • In 2020, COVID-19 pandemic significantly accelerated e-learning in medicine, particularly in gastroenterology
  • Online educational instruments and platforms rapidly adopted to maintain continuity of medical education amidst social-distancing measures → e-learning now solidified into educational fabric
Role of E-Learning in Gastroenterology
  • Specialty rapidly evolving due to endoscopic and pharmacologic advances → gastroenterologists must keep pace with medical knowledge and technology
  • Traditional teaching methods lack flexibility and fail to engage adult learners effectively
  • E-learning advantages: flexible, self-paced, asynchronous access; potential to incorporate flipped classroom, microlearning, collaborative learning, interactive learning
  • Social media platforms can facilitate collaborative learning and knowledge-sharing among gastroenterologists
  • Studies show e-learning has high acceptability, improves knowledge retention, and is more effective than traditional education for medical students and residents
  • Simulation exercises (virtual/physical with partial task trainer integration) augment skills from e-learning modules
2. Key Components of Effective E-Learning Programs
Core Principles and Design Elements
  • Clear objectives: Activities require specific learning objectives and structured curricula informed by needs assessments and learner feedback
  • Interactive design: Engaging learners virtually can be challenging; elements like online polling, chat-based Q&A, case-based discussions enhance engagement
  • Virtual simulations provide practical, hands-on experiences in controlled environments
  • Gamification and scheduled content delivery enhance user interaction with handheld devices (smartphones/tablets)
  • Faculty training: Educators must be proficient in digital tools and online pedagogical strategies to design effective e-learning experiences
Assessments, Feedback, and Content Management
  • Assessments and feedback: Crucial because e-learning differs from traditional methods; platforms can create posttest assessments to identify knowledge gaps
  • Spaced repetition of focused teaching points through bite-sized content (microlearning) addresses gaps
  • Regular assessments support test-enhanced learning, track progress, identify improvement areas → improves knowledge acquisition and retention
  • Content revision and periodic updates: Medical knowledge expands exponentially; e-learning platforms can incorporate advances more rapidly than traditional learning
  • Regular updates based on evaluation data and latest advancements required; must include built-in technical support for software glitches
User Experience and Accessibility
  • User-friendly platforms: Should be easily accessible, intuitive to navigate, provide asynchronous learning opportunities
  • Learners can access content at own pace and convenience → particularly beneficial for medical professionals with varying schedules or different time zones
3. Hybrid Learning Approaches
Definition and Benefits
  • Hybrid learning combines e-learning with traditional face-to-face classroom methods in gastroenterology education
  • Leverages strengths of both asynchronous and synchronous learning to enhance educational experience
  • Has been shown to increase engagement, support diverse learning styles, improve outcomes
Flipped Classroom Strategy
  • Particularly effective in hybrid courses: traditional didactic education reversed → learners receive precourse study materials, class time used for active collaborative learning, group projects, discussions
  • Learners complete precourse self-paced study → then acquire/refine skills/techniques based on that knowledge
  • Postmodule assessments support test-enhanced learning → help learners identify knowledge gaps to focus on during in-person sessions
  • Makes hybrid courses more flexible, learner-centered, effective at consolidating cognitive and technical skills
4. ASGE's E-Learning Platforms: GI Leap
Platform Overview and Capabilities
  • GI Leap (https://www.asge.org/home/education/advanced-education-training/online-learning-gi-leap/gi-leap-lp) is ASGE's advanced e-learning platform designed for optimal learning experiences for gastroenterologists
  • Anchor to ASGE's e-learning offerings; incorporates cutting-edge technology for high-quality educational content
  • Offers accessibility on various devices, including offline capabilities
  • Vast library of educational resources: videos of procedures, webinars, courses
  • ASGE members have easy access to broad range of educational materials with easy navigation and efficient search capabilities
  • Users can track CME and Maintenance of Certification (MOC) credits
5. GESAP and GESAP Mentor Program
Program Description and Structure
  • GESAP (Gastrointestinal Endoscopy Self-Assessment Program; https://www.asge.org/quicklinks/gesap/gesap-mentor) is ASGE's premier educational tool
  • Allows learners to stay current in ever-changing field of endoscopy, gastroenterology, hepatology with latest clinical developments and guidelines highlighted in multimedia, case-based scenarios
  • Currently has 13 modules with several options for individuals to access
Methodology and Learning Approach
  • Uses commercially available interval-reinforcement methodology scientifically proven to improve proficiency (process of increasing level of expertise in specific area as measure of knowledge improvement/retention and ability to apply knowledge to solve problems)
  • Durably changes behaviors with measurable impact on outcomes
  • Spaced-learning, immediate answer feedback, and repetition with knowledge reinforcement → improved engagement and performance
  • Online learning platform provides optimized, seamless experience accessible on any device/system at own pace → supports flexibility for busy learners
  • Uses scenarios in case-based format to make learning relevant to clinical practice
GESAP Mentor Outcomes and Data
  • GESAP Mentor = version used in GI fellowship programs
  • Preliminary data from 16 GI fellowship programs with total of 193 participants: overall proficiency gain for all modules was 66% (Figure 1)
  • Identifies areas of knowledge deficit → programs can focus attention on specific lectures and case-based discussions to improve proficiency
  • Fellows using GESAP Mentor report increased engagement, knowledge retention, and proficiency
  • Ability to identify each fellow's knowledge needs allows fellowship directors to personalize learning
6. STAR Certificate Programs and Hybrid Courses
STAR Program Overview
  • Skills, Training, Assessment, and Reinforcement (STAR) Certificate Programs (https://www.asge.org/home/education/advanced-education-training/star-certificate-programs)
  • Use simulation-based hands-on practice with reflective feedback
  • Foundational learning with e-learning including postmodule assessments to support test-enhanced learning
  • Contextual understanding through didactic and case discussions to teach performance of advanced endoscopic procedures
Curriculum Structure
  • Each STAR program includes 2 sequential training components:
  • Phase 1 (E-learning): Baseline test to gauge participant's knowledge → read expert-curated literature on guidelines/technology → watch instructional videos from ASGE Endoscopic Learning Library (GI Leap) → postcourse test gauges
Phase 2 (Live Course and Assessment)
  • Second phase focuses on technical skill development with hands-on training led by leading subject matter experts at ASGE IT&T Center
  • Includes case-based video panel discussions, video debriefing, concludes with one-on-one skills assessment
  • Upon successful completion of knowledge and skills-based assessment requirements → participants receive STAR Certificate of Completion and transcript
  • Transcript includes: program curriculum details, CME/MOC earned, feedback from hands-on skills assessment
  • Creates comprehensive, learner-centered approach prioritizing competence, self-reflection, clinical readiness → prepares learners for clinical integration into practice
STAR Lower GI EMR Course Effectiveness Data
  • Example of ASGE hybrid, competency-based learning model effectiveness: STAR Lower GI EMR endoscopy course significantly enhanced both cognitive and technical skills in endoscopists training in endoscopic mucosal resection
  • Course directors developed 12 technical milestones for lower GI EMR
  • Cognitive portion: combined online e-learning with in-person didactic sessions
  • Technical component: hands-on training on ex vivo models
  • Cognitive skills measured using 2 separate examinations administered pre- and postcourse completion
  • Technical skills assessed using standardized evaluation tools for lower GI EMR procedures on ex vivo models
Course Outcomes and Statistics
  • 20 participants in 2025 lower GI EMR course
  • Postcourse cognitive skill scores improved significantly: from 69.8% ± 8.5% to 96.1% ± 6.2% (P < .0001) (Figure 2)
  • Proportion of participants rating overall quality of course as "excellent" (highest rating): 92.9%

  • ASGE Annual GI advanced practice provider course: Hybrid format (e-learning, on-demand + live hands-on); offered annually; CME and MOC credits offered; additional cost

  • ASGE first year fellows course: Hybrid format (e-learning, on-demand + live hands-on); offered 4 times per year; CME and MOC credits offered; free to first year trainee members
  • ASGE Golden Scopes: Hybrid on-demand + live component; offered annually; no CME; free to trainee members

8. Challenges in Implementing E-Learning
Technological Barriers and Infrastructure
  • Robust digital infrastructure required to handle high web-based traffic and provide seamless user experiences
  • Ability to provide secure content and regulate user interface in digitally friendly, learner-oriented manner important to avoid advertent/inadvertent abuse or misuse of online platform
  • Solutions: invest in secure, scalable platforms
Educator Preparedness and Training Gaps
  • Educators and teaching faculty traditionally not trained in e-learning methodologies → can lead to ineffective content mirroring traditional didactic teaching
  • Training educators in technical skills and digital tools essential for more effective teaching
Learner Resistance and Engagement
  • Adult learners not used to e-learning may have resistance or apprehension to these platforms
  • Overcome through continued engagement and streamlining of end-user interface
Content Update Challenges
  • Traditional learning resources have publication date; e-learning resources expected to be current or near-current
  • Pace of medical research rapidly accelerating driven by technological advancements and growing understanding of disease
  • Regular content review and updates add to financial/logistical burden → must be tracked and accounted for in e-learning platform budget
Standardization and Quality Control Issues
  • Variability in learning outcomes may lead to learner disengagement in distance learning
  • Regulations associated with e-learning less robust than traditional teaching (typically done through accredited institutions/organizations)
  • Caution required when evaluating e-learning programs → lack of standardization may unintentionally yield quality control concerns for disseminated material
  • Accreditation standards should be maintained to ensure quality and consistency across programs
9. Future Trends in E-Learning for Gastroenterology
Virtual Reality, Augmented, and Mixed Reality Technologies
  • VR, augmented reality, and mixed reality can enhance learning experience by providing immersive, interactive environment
  • Simulate clinical scenarios → allow learners to practice new endoscopic skills in safe, controlled setting
Artificial Intelligence Integration
  • Rise in AI use in learning can be harnessed to adapt and personalize learning to individual's:
  • Learning style
  • Geographic location
  • Practice environment
  • Knowledge gaps
Global Collaboration and Knowledge Sharing
  • Trend in collaborative learning through online peer interactions and global knowledge sharing
  • ASGE Digestibles program: presented via microlearning platform; designed to increase knowledge and retention in convenient, engaging interaction
  • Participants receive 3 rounds of 12-15 questions spaced a few questions at a time over 6 weeks → engage at time that works for their schedule
  • Increased proficiency ratings keep learners coming back for more
  • Has engaged learners from fellows in training to those with more than 10 years of experience, across 60 countries globally
  • Global engagement helps accelerate learning and advancement in science, collaboration → may eventually help improve patient care
10. Conclusions
Transformative Impact of E-Learning
  • E-learning has significantly transformed gastroenterology education by providing flexible, accessible, interactive learning opportunities
ASGE's Leadership and Platform Portfolio

  • GESAP: self-assessment program
  • Hybrid offerings: STAR programs, ASGE Recognized Industry Associate programs
Technology Integration and Learning Outcomes
  • Platforms incorporate advanced technologies including virtual simulations and spaced learning → enhance knowledge retention and proficiency
Challenges and Future Outlook
  • Challenges: technological barriers, standardization issues
  • Future promising with potential of emerging technologies like VR and AI for personalization of education on global scale
