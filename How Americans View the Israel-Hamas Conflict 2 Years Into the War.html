How Americans View the Israel-Hamas Conflict 2 Years Into the War
  • Source: Pew Research Center
  • Publication Date: October 2025
  • Survey Dates: Sept. 22-28, 2025
  • Sample Size: 3,445 U.S. adults
Knowledge Test
  • 1. What percentage of Americans now believe Israel's military operation is 'going too far,' and how has this changed since late 2023?
  • 2. Which political entity (Israeli government, Palestinian Authority, Hamas) receives the highest unfavorable rating from Americans?
  • 3. How do Democrats and Republicans differ in their primary concerns regarding the conflict's impact on civilians versus future security threats?
  • 4. What is the dominant view among Americans regarding the amount of U.S. military assistance to Israel versus humanitarian aid to Palestinian civilians?
  • 5. While views on the Israeli government have worsened, what is the corresponding trend in American opinion of the Israeli *people* since early 2024?
Overall Public Opinion Shift
  • Nearly two years into the conflict, American skepticism of Israel's military operation & government is higher.
  • Large shares of Americans express uncertainty across multiple questions.
Perception of Israel's Military Operation
  • A growing share of Americans believe Israel's military operation against Hamas is excessive.
Trend: 'Going Too Far'
  • The percentage saying Israel is 'going too far' has steadily increased.
  • Sep 2025: 39%
  • Sep 2024: 31%
  • Dec 2023: 27%
Other Views (Sep 2025)
  • Taking about the right approach: 16%
  • Not going far enough: 10%
  • Not sure: 33%
Partisan Divide on Israel's Approach
  • Democrats are significantly more critical of Israel's military actions than Republicans.
Democratic View
  • A strong majority believes Israel is going too far.
  • Sep 2025: 60% say 'going too far' (↑ 10 points from Sep 2024).
  • Other views: 7% 'right approach', 3% 'not far enough'.
Republican View
  • Views are more divided, with a significant portion unsure.
  • Going too far: 19% (↑ from 13% in Sep 2024).
  • Taking right approach: 27%
  • Not going far enough: 18%
  • Not sure: 35%
Opinion of Israeli Government
  • Unfavorable views of the Israeli government have increased.
Trend Data
  • Unfavorable opinion: 59% (↑ from 51% in early 2024).
  • Favorable opinion: 35% (↓ from 41% in 2024).
Assessment of U.S. Response & Aid
  • Focus on the Trump administration's handling of the conflict.
  • Views on military vs. humanitarian aid show an inverse relationship.
Trump Administration's Response
  • More Americans disapprove (42%) than approve (30%) of the administration's response.
  • A significant portion (27%) remains unsure.
Perception of Favoritism
  • A growing share believes President Trump is favoring Israel excessively.
Key Figures (Sep 2025)
  • Favoring Israelis too much: 36% (↑ 5 points from March).
  • Striking the right balance: 23% (↓ 6 points from March).
  • Favoring Palestinians too much: 2%
  • Not sure: 38%
Partisan Divide on Trump's Response
  • Deep partisan divisions exist in evaluating the administration's handling.
Republicans & Leaners
  • Approve: 54%
  • Disapprove: 18%
Democrats & Leaners
  • Approve: 9%
  • Disapprove: 67%
Partisan Divide on Favoritism
  • The share saying Trump favors Israelis too much has risen in both parties.
Democrats
  • Say Trump favors Israelis too much: 57% (↑ from 50% in March).
Republicans
  • Say Trump favors Israelis too much: 18% (↑ from 13% in March).
U.S. Aid to the Region
  • Public opinion on U.S. aid is polarized between military support for Israel and humanitarian support for Palestinians.
Military Assistance to Israel
  • A plurality of Americans believe the U.S. provides too much military aid to Israel.
  • Too much: 33%
  • About the right amount: 23%
  • Not enough: 8%
  • Not sure: 35%
Breakdown by Age
  • Younger Americans are most likely to view aid as excessive.
  • Ages 18-29: 42% say 'too much'.
  • Ages 65+: 24% say 'too much'.
Breakdown by Party
  • Democrats: 49% say 'too much'.
  • Republicans: More likely to say aid is 'about the right amount'.
Humanitarian Aid to Palestinians
  • A plurality believes the U.S. provides insufficient humanitarian aid to civilians in Gaza.
  • Not enough: 35%
  • About the right amount: 20%
  • Too much: 9%
  • Not sure: 35%
Breakdown by Age
  • Ages 18-29: 41% say 'not enough'.
  • Ages 65+: 34% say 'not enough', but are closely split with 31% saying 'right amount'.
Breakdown by Party
  • Democrats: 58% say 'not enough'.
  • Republicans: More likely to say aid is 'about the right amount'.
Public Concerns about the Conflict
  • Widespread concern exists across several aspects of the war.
  • ≥80% of Americans are at least 'somewhat concerned' about starvation, civilian deaths, and hostages.
Top-Tier Concerns
  • Issues where over half of Americans are 'extremely' or 'very' concerned.
Starvation in Gaza
  • 55% are extremely/very concerned.
Palestinian Civilian Deaths
  • 53% are extremely/very concerned about deaths from Israeli military strikes.
Return of Israeli Hostages
  • 51% are extremely/very concerned about hostages not being returned.
Other Major Concerns
  • Issues where about 4-in-10 are 'extremely' or 'very' concerned.
Future Hamas Attacks on Israel
  • 74% are at least somewhat concerned.
Forced Displacement of Palestinians
  • 69% are at least somewhat concerned about Palestinians being forced to leave Gaza.
Partisan Differences in Concern
  • Democrats and Republicans express different levels of concern on specific issues.
Issues of Higher Concern for Democrats
  • Democrats are more likely to be extremely/very concerned about the conflict's impact on Palestinians.
Starvation in Gaza
  • Democrats: 74%
  • Republicans: 39%
Palestinian Civilian Deaths
  • Democrats: 72%
  • Republicans: 37%
Forced Displacement
  • Democrats: 61%
  • Republicans: 21%
Issues of Higher Concern for Republicans
  • Republicans are more concerned about future attacks by Hamas.
Future Hamas Attacks
  • Republicans: 52% extremely/very concerned.
  • Democrats: 39% extremely/very concerned.
Area of Bipartisan Concern
  • Both parties show nearly identical levels of high concern for the Israeli hostages.
Return of Hostages
  • Republicans: 54% extremely/very concerned.
  • Democrats: 51% extremely/very concerned.
Views of Peoples vs. Political Leadership
  • Americans hold much more positive views of Israeli and Palestinian peoples than their political leadership.
  • Views of Hamas are overwhelmingly negative across the board.
Israeli People & Government
  • Views of both the Israeli people and their government have become more negative since early 2024.
Israeli People
  • Favorable: 56% (↓ 8 points from 64% in 2024).
  • Unfavorable: 38%
Partisan Gap
  • Republicans: 67% favorable.
  • Democrats: Split at 48% favorable / 48% unfavorable.
Israeli Government
  • Unfavorable: 59% (↑ from 51% in 2024).
  • Favorable: 35% (↓ from 41% in 2024).
Partisan Gap
  • Republicans: 55% favorable.
  • Democrats: 18% favorable (77% unfavorable).
Palestinian People & Leadership
  • Views of Palestinian people and leadership have remained relatively stable.
Palestinian People
  • Favorable: 52% (stable).
  • Unfavorable: 41%
Partisan Gap
  • Democrats: 70% favorable.
  • Republicans: 37% favorable.
Palestinian Authority
  • Unfavorable: 68% (stable).
  • Favorable: 24%
Partisan Gap
  • Republicans: 82% unfavorable.
  • Democrats: 56% unfavorable.
Hamas
  • Unfavorable: 84% (stable).
  • Favorable: 10%
Bipartisan Consensus
  • Overwhelmingly negative views from both parties.
  • Republicans: 89% unfavorable.
  • Democrats: 82% unfavorable.
