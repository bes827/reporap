What is new in Chicago Classification version 4.0?
  • Source: Neurogastroenterology & Motility 2020;00:e14053
  • Authors: Rena Yadlapati, John E. Pandolfino, Mark R. Fox, Albert J. Bredenoord, Peter J. Kahrilas
1. Historical Evolution of the Chicago Classification
  • Originated in Paris 2007 (idea by JEP, AJB, MF) → international HRM Working Group.
  • First “Chicago Classification” description: Fox & Bredenoord 2007 (CCv0.5).
  • Inaugural International HRM Working Group meeting: San Diego 2008 → CCv1.0 published 2009.
  • Ray E. Clouse (1951‑2007) pioneered esophageal pressure topography color plots.
2. Rationale & Objectives of the Classification Updates
  • Aim: standardized interpretation of clinical HRM worldwide.
  • Goal: “speak the same language,” eliminate competing nomenclatures (“Tower of Babylon”).
  • Adopt metrics validated against independent functional tests (e.g., radiology).
  • Hierarchical diagnostic system: prioritize EGJ outflow disorders > major peristalsis disorders > minor peristalsis abnormalities.
3. Overview of Chicago Classification v4.0 Development Process
  • International HRM Working Group: 52 experts from 20 countries, 5 continents, 6 societies.
  • Divided into 7 sub‑groups (standardized protocol, achalasia, EGJOO, DES, hypercontractile esophagus, IEM, EGJ barrier metrics).
  • Consensus method: RAND Appropriateness Method + online surveys → equal contribution.
  • Evidence grading: GRADE assessment by external reviewers; most literature low/very‑low quality but unbiased review ensured.
4. Key Modifications Introduced in CCv4.0 (compared with v3.0)
  • EGJ outflow obstruction (EGJOO): requires additional manometric & non‑manometric data for actionable diagnosis.
  • Clinical relevance requirement: EGJOO, distal esophageal spasm (DES), hypercontractile esophagus must be accompanied by dysphagia and/or non‑cardiac chest pain to be considered clinically relevant.
  • Standardized HRM protocol: include supine + upright wet swallows; add multiple rapid swallows (supine) & rapid drink challenge (upright); optional solid swallows, post‑prandial testing, pharmacologic provocation for select conditions.
  • Ineffective esophageal motility (IEM) definition tightened: >70% ineffective swallows OR ≥50% failed swallows; now includes fragmented peristalsis.
  • Classification simplification: no longer distinguishes major vs. minor motility disorders; separates only disorders of EGJ outflow and disorders of peristalsis.
5. Standardized HRM Protocol (v4.0)
  • Recommended core: 10 supine wet swallows + 10 upright wet swallows.
  • Add supine multiple rapid swallow (MRS) and upright rapid drink challenge (RDC).
  • Optional extensions: solid test swallows, post‑prandial studies, pharmacologic provocation.
  • Limited protocol (only supine or only upright) acceptable for clear‑cut achalasia or aspiration risk.
5.1 Rationale & Benefits
  • Improves diagnostic accuracy for EGJOO & hypercontractile patterns.
  • Enhances inter‑lab collaboration via uniform maneuvers.
  • Allows supportive data (intrabolus pressure, response to RDC) to raise confidence.
6. Clinical Relevance of Manometric Patterns
  • EGJOO, DES, hypercontractile esophagus: must have obstructive symptoms (dysphagia ± non‑cardiac chest pain).
  • Patterns without symptom correlation are marked with asterisks (*) → “unclear clinical relevance.”
  • Supports hierarchical approach: EGJ dysfunction prioritized over peristaltic abnormalities.
7. Updates to Specific Esophageal Motor Disorders
7.1 Achalasia (unchanged core criteria)
  • Elevated median IRP (supine / upright) + 100% failed peristalsis.
  • Type II: panesophageal pressurization ≥20% swallows (distinction from type I arbitrary).
  • Type III: spasm (DL <4.5 s, DCI >450 mmHg·s·cm) in ≥20% swallows; 100% absent peristalsis now defined as failed or spastic swallows.
7.2 EGJ Outflow Obstruction (EGJOO) – New Definition
  • Elevated median IRP both supine & upright.
  • ≥20% swallows with intrabolus pressurization, but intact peristalsis (no achalasia).
  • Diagnosis inconclusive → must have dysphagia and/or non‑cardiac chest pain and confirmatory non‑HRM test (TBE or FLIP).
  • Ancillary maneuvers (rapid drink challenge, solid swallows, pharmacologic provocation) may strengthen confidence.
7.2.1 Phenotypes of EGJOO
  • EGJOO with spastic features: ≥20% swallows show premature contractions; may approach type III achalasia.
  • EGJOO with hypercontractile features: reactive hyper‑contraction to obstruction or primary hypercontractility involving LES.
  • EGJOO with ineffective motility or normal peristalsis: likely physiologic/reflux; cautious treatment, confirm with adjunctive testing.
7.3 Disorders of Peristalsis (IRP normal or EGJOO criteria not met)
  • Includes absent contractility, DES, hypercontractile esophagus, IEM.
7.3.1 Absent Contractility
  • Normal median IRP + 100% failed peristalsis.
  • If IRP at upper limit of normal → exclude achalasia with rapid drink challenge, solid test meal, TBE/FLIP.
7.3.2 Distal Esophageal Spasm (DES)
  • Normal median IRP + ≥20% premature contractions and dysphagia/non‑cardiac chest pain required for relevance.
  • Often overlaps type III achalasia; may represent reflux‑induced secondary spasm → treat reflux first.
7.3.3 Hypercontractile Esophagus (Jackhammer subtype)
  • Normal median IRP + ≥20% hypercontractile swallows and obstructive symptoms required.
  • Subtypes: repetitive prolonged contractions, single‑peak hypercontractile, vigorous LES after‑contraction; jackhammer pattern most symptomatic.
7.3.4 Ineffective Esophageal Motility (IEM) – Revised Definition
  • >70% ineffective swallows or ≥50% failed swallows.
  • Ineffective swallow now includes fragmented peristalsis; fragmented peristalsis no longer a separate motor disorder.
8. Therapeutic Guidance & Future Directions
  • EGJOO phenotypes guide POEM extension proximally; confirm obstruction physiology before myotomy.
  • Opioid‑induced dysmotility excluded from primary CC definitions – cease opioid first.
  • Antireflux therapy preferred when DES secondary to GERD.
9. Summary of Chicago Classification v4.0
  • EGJOO actionable only after HRM + non‑HRM confirmation.
  • EGJOO, DES, hypercontractile esophagus require dysphagia/non‑cardiac chest pain for relevance.
  • Protocol: supine + upright swallows; add MRS & rapid drink challenge; optional solids/post‑prandial/provocation.
  • IEM stricter: >70% ineffective or ≥50% failed swallows; includes fragmented peristalsis.
  • Classification now disorders of EGJ outflow vs disorders of peristalsis only.
