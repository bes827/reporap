üíä Effects of GLP-1 Receptor Agonists (GLP-1RAs) in GI and Endoscopy
  • - Multiple meta-analyses highlight significant effects of GLP-1RAs on endoscopy outcomes and GI adverse events.
Endoscopy Outcomes (Singh et al.)
  • - Analysis of 23 studies involving 77,152 patients (4,449 on GLP-1RA).
‚¨ÜÔ∏è Increased Risk Events
  • - Patients on GLP-1RAs demonstrated significantly higher odds of:
Retained Gastric Contents
  • - OR: 15.39 (P < .01).

Aborted Procedures
  • - OR: 13.86 (P < .01).


üö´ No Significant Difference
  • - Despite increased gastric contents, there was no significant difference in:
Aspiration Events
  • - OR: 21.06 (P = .24).

Bowel Preparation Quality
  • - Subjective assessment showed no difference (OR: 0.94).



Upper GI Endoscopy Specifics (Baig et al.)
  • - Findings were similar to the Singh et al. analysis.
‚¨áÔ∏è Reduced Retained Contents
  • - A significant reduction in retained gastric contents was observed when upper endoscopy and colonoscopy were performed on the same day versus upper endoscopy alone.
  • - OR: 0.28 (P < .001).


Adverse GI Events (Chiang et al.)
  • - Meta-analysis of 55 randomized controlled trials (106,395 participants).
Increased Risk vs. Placebo
  • - GLP-1RAs increased the risk of:
Cholelithiasis
  • - RR: 1.46 (1.09-1.97).

GERD
  • - RR: 2.19 (1.48-3.25).


Subgroup Analysis
  • - Effects were more pronounced in trials with:
  • - Individuals with obesity or steatotic liver disease.
  • - Use of weight-loss-inducing GLP-1RAs.
  • - High-dose formulations.
  • - Note: These subgroup effects were not statistically significant.



ü©∏ Endoscopic Band Ligation (EBL) in Gastric Antral Vascular Ectasia (GAVE)
  • - A meta-analysis by Garg et al. compared EBL with the more widely used Argon Plasma Coagulation (APC).
EBL vs. APC: Key Outcomes
  • - Analysis of 10 studies with 476 subjects.
  • - Overall results were overwhelmingly in support of EBL.
‚úÖ Superior Efficacy of EBL
  • - Eradication Rate: EBL 88.6% vs. APC 57.9% (RR 1.52, P < .001).
  • - Bleeding Recurrence: EBL 6.6% vs. APC 39.7%.
  • - GAVE Recurrence: EBL 7.3% vs. APC 38.5%.

‚¨áÔ∏è Improved Clinical Metrics with EBL
  • - Fewer treatment sessions required with EBL.
  • - Lower transfusion requirements with EBL.
  • - Fewer hospitalizations with EBL.

‚ö†Ô∏è Adverse Events
  • - Adverse event rate was higher with EBL, but none were considered 'reportable' by ASGE lexicon.
  • - EBL: 16.8%
  • - APC: 9.3%

Limitations
  • - A major limitation was the inclusion of observational retrospective studies.



üí® Hemostatic Powder in Nonvariceal GI Bleeding
  • - Liu et al. evaluated the efficacy of hemostatic powders versus conventional endoscopic therapy (CET).
Efficacy based on Bleeding Etiology
  • - Meta-analysis of 8 studies (653 patients).
Malignancy-Related Bleeding
  • - Hemostatic powder monotherapy was significantly superior to CET.
Initial Hemostasis
  • - Improved rate with RR 1.5 (1.28-1.75).

30-Day Recurrent Bleeding
  • - Reduced rate with RR 0.32 (0.12-0.86).


Non-Malignant Bleeding
  • - Hemostatic powders demonstrated similar outcomes to CET for:
  • - Initial hemostasis.
  • - 30-day recurrent bleeding rates.

Trial Sequential Analysis
  • - Confirmed the robust results for malignancy-related causes.
  • - Concluded that the relative efficacy in non-malignant bleeding remains unresolved.



üî• Endoscopic Antireflux Therapy for GERD
  • - Angeramo et al. compared Anti-Reflux Mucosectomy (ARMS) vs. Radiofrequency Ablation (Stretta procedure).
ARMS vs. Stretta: Efficacy & Safety
  • - Systematic review of 66 studies (3,767 patients).
  • - Stretta group (n=3074) was much larger than ARMS group (n=693).
‚úÖ Clinical Efficacy: Similar Outcomes
  • - Both modalities demonstrated similar effectiveness.
Patient Satisfaction
  • - ARMS: 77%
  • - Stretta: 65%

Reduced PPI Use
  • - ARMS: Reduction from 99% to 48%.
  • - Stretta: Reduction from 100% to 40%.

GERD-HRQL Scores
  • - Both showed significant improvement.


‚ö†Ô∏è Safety Profile: Stretta is Safer
  • - ARMS demonstrated greater overall morbidity: 25% vs. 17% for Stretta (P < .001).
Risk of Stricture
  • - Significantly greater with ARMS.
  • - OR: 13.03.

Risk of Bleeding
  • - Significantly greater with ARMS.
  • - OR: 13.16.

Risk of Perforation
  • - Significantly greater with ARMS.
  • - OR: 13.03.


Limitations & Considerations
  • - Primarily retrospective cohort studies.
  • - Grossly unequal sample sizes.
  • - ARMS is a newer procedure with an unknown learning curve.



üî¨ Endoscopy in Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD)
  • - Shehab et al. performed a network meta-analysis on endoscopic dysplasia detection technologies.
Comparison of Dysplasia Detection Technologies
  • - Analysis of 25 studies (4,837 patients, 850 dysplastic lesions).
üèÜ Top Performer: Dye-Based Chromoendoscopy (DCE)
  • - In per-lesion analysis, DCE ranked highest (83%).
DCE vs. HD-WLE
  • - DCE was superior to high-definition white-light endoscopy.
  • - OR: 1.78 (1.06-3).

DCE vs. Other Advanced Techniques
  • - No significant differences were found in head-to-head comparisons with:
  • - Narrow-band imaging (NBI).
  • - HD-WLE with segmental reinspection.
  • - Chromoendoscopy-guided endomicroscopy.


Subgroup: Ulcerative Colitis
  • - DCE again ranked highest in per-lesion analysis.
DCE vs. NBI
  • - DCE was superior to narrow-band imaging in this subgroup.
  • - OR: 1.69 (1.03-2.77).


Noteworthy Technique: HD-WLE with Segmental Reinspection
  • - Showed interesting utility, but this finding stemmed from only one study performed at expert centers.
