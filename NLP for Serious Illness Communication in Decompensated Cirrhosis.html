Clinical Context: Serious Illness Communication (SIC) in Decompensated Cirrhosis (DC)
  • - Patients with Decompensated Cirrhosis (DC) have a poor prognosis, with a median survival of under two years without a liver transplant.
  • - DC is the 9th leading cause of death in the United States.
  • - Despite poor prognosis, patients often receive high-intensity end-of-life care and rarely utilize palliative or hospice services.
What is Serious Illness Communication (SIC)?
  • - A process where clinicians support patients and families in understanding their illness, prognosis, and care planning.
  • - Goal: Ensure medical care is consistent with the patient's informed preferences, goals, and values.
Key Components of SIC
  • - Goals of Care (GOC) conversations.
  • - Preferences for end-of-life care, including code status limitations.
  • - Involvement of specialist palliative care and hospice services.

Benefits of Early & Effective SIC
  • - Improved patient and family satisfaction with communication and care.
  • - Reduced emotional distress.
  • - Reduced receipt of burdensome and potentially non-beneficial interventions.


Challenges in Measuring SIC
  • - SIC documentation is often unstructured and difficult to track in the Electronic Health Record (EHR).
  • - Manual chart review is the traditional gold standard but is inefficient, time-consuming, and resource-intensive.
  • - Administrative billing codes are poor proxies for actual SIC conversations.


Study: Using NLP to Identify SIC Documentation
  • - Objective: To develop and evaluate a semi-automated Natural Language Processing (NLP) approach to identify SIC documentation in clinical notes for patients with DC.
Methods
  • - A single-center, retrospective cohort study.
Study Population
  • - N = 196 unique adult patients with DC who were evaluated for liver transplant and subsequently died.
  • - Study period: 1/1/2010 to 12/31/2017, with deaths by 6/30/2018.
  • - All inpatient and outpatient notes from one year until three days prior to death were extracted from the EHR.

NLP Keyword Library Development
  • - An initial keyword library for cancer patients was adapted for DC using an iterative process.
  • - Used ClinicalRegex, an open-source NLP software.
  • - A consensus panel of experts refined the library.
Four SIC Domains
  • - 1. Goals of Care (GOC) conversations.
  • - 2. Code status limitations.
  • - 3. Specialist palliative care involvement.
  • - 4. Hospice assessment.

DC-Specific Adaptations (See Table 1)
  • - Added keywords for DC-specific decisions: dialysis, pressors, ventilation, and transplant goals.
  • - Removed 7 keywords and added 45 new inclusion keywords.
  • - Example Removals: 'supportive care' was ambiguous in DC; 'PC' often meant 'primary care', not 'palliative care'.


Performance Evaluation
  • - NLP performance was compared to a gold standard of manual chart review.
  • - Gold standard dataset: 15 randomly selected decedents with 1,066 clinical notes.
  • - Metrics included precision, recall (sensitivity), accuracy, and the F1 score.


Results
  • - The study included 196 unique decedents with 14,062 clinical notes.
Patient Characteristics (See Table 2)
  • - Average age at death: 57.7 years.
  • - 62.2% male and 81.1% White.
  • - 55.6% had been listed for liver transplantation.
  • - Median MELD-Na score at listing was 20.

NLP Performance vs. Gold Standard (See Table 3)
  • - NLP was significantly more efficient than manual review.
  • - NLP: 6.8 minutes per patient.
  • - Manual Review: 41.5 minutes per patient (~6 times faster).
Performance Scores (F1)
  • - GOC conversations: F1 score of 1.0 (perfect agreement).
  • - Specialist palliative care: F1 score of 1.0.
  • - Hospice assessment: F1 score of 1.0.
  • - Code status limitations: F1 score of 0.91 (NLP outperformed manual review, identifying one instance missed manually).


Overall SIC Documentation Findings (N=196)
  • - A striking 48% of patients had NO SIC documentation in any domain prior to the last 3 days of life.
  • - 52.0% (101 patients) had documentation in at least one SIC domain.
Breakdown by SIC Domain (See Figure 2)
  • - GOC conversations: 41.8% of patients.
  • - Code status limitations: 37.8%.
  • - Specialist palliative care involvement: 26.0%.
  • - Hospice assessment: 21.9%.




Discussion and Clinical Implications
  • - This is the first study to apply a semi-automated NLP approach for SIC in patients with DC.
  • - The DC-specific keyword library is a key contribution, including language around critical decisions like dialysis and transplant.
Key Finding: NLP is Superior to Manual Review
  • - NLP is more efficient (~6x faster).
  • - NLP is as accurate (or more so) than manual chart review.
  • - This method enables large-scale analysis for quality improvement and multisite clinical trials.

Key Finding: Significant Deficits in SIC
  • - Nearly half of this decedent cohort had no documented SIC before the last 3 days of life.
  • - This confirms prior studies showing underutilization of palliative and hospice care in DC.
  • - Highlights a critical need for interventions to promote early and effective SIC.

Barriers to SIC in DC
  • - Unpredictable illness trajectory.
  • - Limited hepatology clinician training in SIC.
  • - Cultural factors and competing demands for clinician time.
  • - Perception that transplant care and palliative care are contradictory rather than complementary.

Limitations of the Study
  • - Single-center study: Generalizability may require adaptation at other institutions.
  • - Documentation-dependent: Cannot capture verbal communication not recorded in the EHR.
  • - Presence, not quality: NLP captures the presence of documentation, not the quality of the conversation.
  • - Semi-automated: Still requires human annotators for review.

Future Directions
  • - Validation in multi-center, contemporary samples across different healthcare systems.
  • - Exploring fully automated tools like Large Language Models (LLMs).
  • - Assessing the impact of ambient scribes on NLP performance.


Conclusion
  • - An NLP keyword library was successfully developed to identify SIC documentation in clinical notes for patients with DC.
  • - NLP-assisted chart review is more efficient and as accurate as manual chart review.
  • - This method can be used at scale for quality improvement initiatives and clinical trials in cirrhosis care.
