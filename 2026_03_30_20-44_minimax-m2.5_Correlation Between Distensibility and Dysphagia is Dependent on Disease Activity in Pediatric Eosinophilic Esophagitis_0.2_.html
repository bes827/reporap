Correlation Between Distensibility and Dysphagia is Dependent on Disease Activity in Pediatric Eosinophilic Esophagitis
  • Source: American Journal of Gastroenterology, Manuscript Number: AJG-26-0612
  • Topic: Pediatric Eosinophilic Esophagitis (EoE) - Functional Lumen Imaging Probe (FLIP) Assessment
  • Author(s): Not specified in the provided text
1. Study Overview and Methods
1.1. Study Design and Population
  • Prospective cohort study at Lurie Children's Hospital (1/2021-3/2024)
  • Patients: 2-21 years old undergoing upper endoscopy with diagnosed or suspected EoE
  • Final cohort: 69 patients (61 EoE, 8 non-EoE controls)
  • Mean age: 13±4 years
  • Female: 28%
1.2. Patient Demographics and Comorbidities
  • Race/ethnicity: 83% White, 0% Black, 9% Asian, 7% Hispanic, 9% Other
  • Comorbidities:
  • Asthma: 38%
  • Eczema: 51%
  • Allergic rhinitis: 59%
  • IgE-mediated food allergy: 55%
  • Gastroesophageal reflux (GERD): 45%
1.3. Assessment Tools
  • FLIP (Functional lumen imaging probe): Assessed diameter at:
  • Distensibility plateau (DP)
  • Esophagogastric junction (EGJ)
  • PEESS (Pediatric Eosinophilic Esophagitis Symptom Survey): Completed prior to endoscopy
  • PedsQL 4.0: Generic and EoE modules for QoL assessment
1.4. Statistical Methods
  • Mann-Whitney test: Compare DP/EGJ diameter by disease status and fibrostenosis
  • Pearson correlation: Between DP/EGJ diameter and PEESS/QoL scores
  • General linear model: Assess DP as predictor of PEESS Dysphagia with control for age, eosinophilia, and endoscopic severity
2. EoE Disease Characteristics
2.1. Disease Status Distribution
  • EoE patients: 61/69 (88%)
  • Active EoE: 37/61 (61%)
  • Remission: 24/61 (39%)
  • Fibrostenosis (rings and/or stricture): 30/61 (49%)
2.2. Histologic and Endoscopic Findings
  • Mean peak EOS/HPF: 35±35
  • iEREFS (inflammatory Endoscopic Reference Score): 7.3±6.9
  • BZH severity (basal zone hyperplasia): 1.5±1.0
3. Distensibility Plateau (DP) Findings
3.1. DP in EoE vs Controls
  • Control: 17.0±1.8 mm
  • EoE: 14.5±2.7 mm
  • p=0.031 (significant reduction in EoE vs controls)
3.2. DP by Disease Activity
  • No significant differences in DP between Active EoE vs Remission groups
3.3. DP by Fibrostenosis Status
  • Fibrostenotic: 12.8±2.1 mm
  • Non-fibrostenotic: 16.2±2.0 mm
  • p<0.001 (significant reduction in fibrostenotic patients)
4. Esophagogastric Junction (EGJ) Findings
4.1. EGJ in EoE vs Controls
  • Control: 17.5±2.8 mm
  • EoE: 14.7±3.3 mm
  • p=0.07 (trend only, not significant)
4.2. EGJ by Fibrostenosis Status
  • Fibrostenotic: 13.6±2.5 mm
  • Non-fibrostenotic: 15.8±3.6 mm
  • p=0.020 (significant reduction in fibrostenotic patients)
4.3. Correlation Between DP and EGJ
  • r=0.56, p<0.001 (moderate correlation)
  • Suggests impact on DP and EGJ from EoE may not be uniform
5. Symptom Correlations with Distensibility
5.1. Overall Correlations (All EoE Patients)
  • DP vs PEESS Composite: r=-0.26, p=0.044 (weak inverse)
  • DP vs PEESS Dysphagia: r=-0.31, p=0.016 (moderate inverse)
  • EGJ diameter: No significant correlations with symptom scores
5.2. Correlations by Disease Activity
5.2.1. Active EoE Patients
  • DP vs PEESS Dysphagia: r=-0.34, p=0.041 (moderate inverse)
  • DP vs PEESS Child Dysphagia: r=-0.46, p=0.016 (moderate inverse)
  • No significant correlations with EGJ diameter
5.2.2. Remission EoE Patients
  • No significant correlations between DP and symptom scores
5.3. Multivariable Model Results
  • DP independently predicted PEESS Dysphagia after adjusting for:
  • Age
  • Eosinophil count
  • Basal zone hyperplasia severity
  • Inflammatory EREFS (sum of proximal, mid, distal esophagus)
  • Effect size: For each 1 mm decrease in DP → PEESS Dysphagia score ↑ by 3.1 points (p=0.042)
6. Quality of Life (QoL) Findings
6.1. QoL by Disease Activity
  • Active EoE vs Remission differences observed in:
  • EoE QoL Child Symptoms: Active 65±17 vs Remission 79±13, p=0.005
  • EoE QoL Child Worry: Active 76±17 vs Remission 86±15, p=0.035
  • EoE QoL Child Food Eating: Active 89±13 vs Remission 76±16, p=0.028
6.2. QoL Correlations with Distensibility
  • Overall: No correlations seen between DP/EGJ and QoL composite or domain scores
6.3. QoL by Disease Activity - Stratified Analysis
  • Remission patients: DP had moderate inverse correlation with PedsQL Child School score (r=-0.47, p=0.025)
  • Remission patients: DP correlated with PedsQL-EoE Symptom Domain 2 (r=0.39, p=0.028)
  • Active EoE: No significant QoL correlations with DP or EGJ
7. Symptom Scores by Disease Activity (PEESS)
7.1. Active EoE vs Remission Comparisons
  • PEESS Composite: Active 33±15 vs Remission 22±14, p=0.021
  • PEESS Child Composite: Active 32±12 vs Remission 18±13, p<0.001
  • PEESS Child Dysphagia: Active 42±21 vs Remission 26±23, p=0.022
  • PEESS Child GERD: Active 21±15 vs Remission 10±12, p=0.007
  • PEESS Child Nausea/Vomiting: Active 24±23 vs Remission 9±14, p=0.013
  • PEESS Parent GERD: Active 22±18 vs Remission 10±13, p=0.007
7.2. Symptom Scores by Fibrostenosis
  • PedsQL Parent Emotional Function: Fibrostenotic 82±19 vs Non-fibrostenotic 72±19, p=0.036
  • PEESS Child Pain: Fibrostenotic 19±19 vs Non-fibrostenotic 32±21, p=0.029
  • No differences in PEESS composite or PedsQL-EoE/Generic composite scores
8. Key Study Limitations
  • Prior treatment: Patients who had undergone prior treatment for EoE were included (could impact symptoms)
  • Sample size: Adequately powered for moderate correlations; limited ability to detect weaker associations
  • FLIP procedure: Performed under general anesthesia (sevoflurane may impact contractile response, though DP likely unaffected)
  • Generalizability: Single-center cohort with limited racial/ethnic diversity
  • Validation needed: Larger, multicenter pediatric population required
9. Study Conclusions
9.1. Primary Findings
  • DP is significantly reduced in pediatric EoE patients vs controls (p=0.03)
  • DP is significantly reduced in fibrostenotic EoE (p<0.001)
  • EGJ diameter reduced in fibrostenotic EoE (p=0.02)
9.2. Key Correlation
  • DP correlates with dysphagia only in patients with active EoE
  • EGJ diameter shows no correlation with symptoms or QoL metrics
9.3. Clinical Implications
  • Dysphagia burden is driven by interaction between:
  • Impaired distensibility (DP)
  • Inflammation (active disease)
  • DP independently predicts dysphagia after adjusting for inflammatory burden
  • Inflammation acts as contextual modifier rather than independent driver of symptoms
9.4. Summary Statement

> "The burden of dysphagia is driven by an interaction between distensibility and inflammation in pediatric EoE."
