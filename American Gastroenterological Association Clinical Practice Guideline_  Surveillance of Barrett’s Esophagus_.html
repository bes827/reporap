American Gastroenterological Association Clinical Practice Guideline: Surveillance of Barrett’s Esophagus
  • Source: American Gastroenterological Association (AGA) Guideline Document.
Knowledge Test
  • Test your knowledge before you begin:
  • 1. What is the annual risk of progression to HGD/EAC in patients with short-segment BE (<3 cm)?
  • 2. What is the pooled incidence of HGD/EAC in patients with columnar lined esophagus <1 cm with intestinal metaplasia?
  • 3. What is the pooled rate of progression to HGD/EAC among NDBE patients with aberrant p53 expression?
  • 4. What percentage of EACs are categorized as Post-Endoscopy Esophageal Adenocarcinoma (PEEC)?
  • 5. What is the failure rate of anti-reflux surgery at 5 years?
Introduction & Health Problem
  • Barrett’s Esophagus (BE) is the only identifiable precursor to Esophageal Adenocarcinoma (EAC).
  • EAC has a dismal 5-year survival rate of approximately 20%.
  • BE progresses to EAC in a stepwise fashion: Low-Grade Dysplasia (LGD) ---> High-Grade Dysplasia (HGD) ---> Invasive EAC.
Goal of Surveillance
  • Primary Goal: Early detection of BE-related neoplasia (dysplasia and early-stage cancer).
  • Secondary Goals:
  • - Enable treatment with Endoscopic Eradication Therapy (EET).
  • - Reduce morbidity/mortality from esophagectomy.
  • - Ultimately prevent EAC mortality.
Surveillance in Non-Dysplastic Barrett’s Esophagus (NDBE)
  • Recommendation: In patients with NDBE, the AGA suggests performing endoscopic surveillance compared to no surveillance.
  • (Conditional recommendation, low certainty).
Evidence: Benefits of Surveillance
  • EAC-Specific Mortality: Surveillance is associated with a significant reduction.
  • - Pooled RR: 0.60 (95% CI 0.50, 0.71) from observational studies.
  • - Absolute risk reduction: 231 fewer deaths per 1,000.
  • Overall Survival: The BOSS trial (RCT) showed no significant difference.
  • - HR: 0.95 (95% CI 0.82, 1.10).
  • Early-Stage Disease Detection: Surveillance significantly increases detection of early-stage EAC/HGD.
  • - BOSS Trial RR: 2.82 (95% CI 1.73, 4.56).
  • - Absolute difference: 21 more cases of early-stage EAC/HGD per 1,000.
Evidence: Harms of Surveillance
  • Serious adverse events from endoscopy are rare.
  • - Bleeding rate: 7.9 per 10,000 persons.
  • - Perforation rate: 0.4 per 10,000 individuals.
  • - 7-day infection rate: 30 per 10,000 individuals.
Discussion & Rationale
  • The recommendation relies heavily on observational studies due to limitations of the BOSS trial.
  • BOSS Trial Limitations:
  • - Likely underpowered for mortality outcomes.
  • - High rate of endoscopy (59%) in the 'at need' control arm, causing contamination.
  • - Nearly 25% of patients lacked intestinal metaplasia, limiting generalizability.
Implementation: Surveillance Intervals
  • Suggested interval for NDBE is every 3 years if a high-quality exam was performed.
  • Interval may be extended to every 5 years in lower-risk patients (e.g., short-segment BE).
Rationale for Intervals
  • Based on modeling studies and cost-effectiveness analyses.
  • Optimal intervals in the U.S. are every 2 to 5 years for NDBE.
  • Shorter intervals are suggested for men than women.
Post-Endoscopy Esophageal Neoplasia (PEEN/PEEC)
  • High prevalence of missed dysplasia/cancer on index exams.
  • Pooled analysis reports PEEC of 17% in patients diagnosed as NDBE on index exam.
  • If index exam quality is questionable, consider repeat endoscopy within 1 year.
Implementation: Risk Stratification
  • Surveillance intervals can be modified based on clinical risk factors.
BE Length
  • The risk factor with the greatest evidence.
  • Annual risk of progression to EAC is significantly lower for short-segment (<3 cm) vs. long-segment BE (0.06% vs. 0.31%).
  • Risk of HGD/EAC doubles for every 4 cm increase in length.
Other Clinical Factors
  • Smoking: Associated with ~1.5x greater odds of progressing.
  • Male Sex: Associated with approximately double the risk of progressing.
  • Increasing BMI: OR of 1.06 per 5 kg/m² increment.
Progression in Barrett’s Esophagus (PIB) Score
  • Combines risk factors: Male sex (9 points), smoking (5 points), BE length (1 point/cm), confirmed LGD (11 points).
  • A low-risk score (<11 points) had an annual progression risk of 0.13% - 0.2%.
Implementation: Discontinuation of Surveillance
  • Consider discontinuation based on age and medical comorbidities.
  • Optimal discontinuation age ranges from <65 to 83 years, depending on sex and comorbidities.
  • Initiate conversation about discontinuation around age 75 in a healthy male.
  • ESGE recommends cessation at age 75 if no prior history of dysplasia.
Optimal Imaging Strategy: Chromoendoscopy (CE)
  • Recommendation: In patients undergoing screening or surveillance, the AGA recommends using a combination of high-definition white light endoscopy (HD-WLE) plus chromoendoscopy compared to WLE alone.
  • (Strong recommendation, moderate quality of evidence).
Evidence: Benefits of Chromoendoscopy
  • Detection of HGD/EAC: CE + WLE significantly increases detection.
  • - RR: 1.20 (95% CI 1.03-1.4).
  • - Absolute increase: 33 more cases per 1,000 patients.
  • Detection of Any Dysplasia (LGD/HGD/EAC):
  • - RR: 1.16 (95% CI 1.07-1.27).
  • - Absolute increase: 61 more cases per 1,000 patients.
Subgroup Analysis (General Surveillance Population)
  • Restricting analysis to studies reflecting a general BE surveillance population showed a stronger effect for HGD/EAC detection.
  • - RR for HGD/EAC detection: 1.55 (95% CI 1.05-2.29).
Evidence: Harms of Chromoendoscopy
  • Generally well-tolerated with no significant adverse events reported in studies.
  • Theoretical concerns include allergic reactions to dyes and potential DNA damage from methylene blue.
Implementation Considerations
  • Choice of Modality: Virtual (e.g., NBI) or dye-based (e.g., acetic acid) CE should be based on endoscopist/center expertise.
  • - Both NBI and acetic acid CE meet acceptable performance thresholds for dysplasia detection.
  • Adjunct, Not Substitute: CE-directed biopsies should be used as an adjunct to, not a substitute for, a structured biopsy protocol.
Future Directions: Artificial Intelligence (AI)
  • AI platforms (CADe/CADx) are being assessed to target neoplastic areas for sampling.
  • One study showed a CADe system was superior to endoscopists in detecting neoplasia in images (90% vs. 74%) and videos (91% vs. 67%).
Core Principles of a High-Quality Examination
  • A meticulous, high-quality endoscopic exam is crucial for accurate staging and minimizing missed neoplasia.
Structured Biopsy Protocol (Seattle Protocol)
  • Recommendation: All patients with suspected or established BE should be sampled using a structured biopsy protocol.
  • Protocol: Includes targeted biopsies from any visible lesions AND random 4-quadrant biopsies every 2 cm (if no prior dysplasia) or every 1 cm (if history of dysplasia).
  • Evidence: Pooled data showed a higher dysplasia detection rate with Seattle protocol vs. non-protocolized biopsies (19.1% vs. 2.6%).
Limitations & Adherence
  • Adherence rates can be suboptimal, as low as 49%.
  • Longer BE segments are associated with lower adherence, despite these patients benefiting most.
Intra-procedure Best Practices
  • Mucosal Visualization: Use distal cap, insufflation/desufflation, and mucosal cleansing.
  • Landmark Identification: Accurately measure and document squamocolumnar junction, GEJ, and diaphragmatic hiatus.
  • Inspection Time: Spend adequate time inspecting the BE segment. One study suggests >1 minute per 1 cm of Barrett's mucosa is associated with higher detection of suspicious lesions.
Standardized Documentation
  • Prague C&M Classification: To describe the circumferential (C) and maximal (M) extent of columnar mucosa.
  • Paris Classification: To describe the macroscopic appearance of any visible superficial lesion.
  • Los Angeles (LA) Classification: To grade and report erosive esophagitis.
Handling Severe Esophagitis
  • In the presence of active LA Grade C or D esophagitis, repeat surveillance endoscopy after at least six weeks of optimized anti-reflux therapy.
  • However, perform detailed inspection and targeted biopsies of any suspicious lesions even with severe esophagitis.
Management of Dysplasia & Indefinite for Dysplasia (IND)
  • Referral: Patients with BE-related neoplasia (including LGD and IND) should be referred to high-volume endoscopists and expert pathologists.
  • Confirmation: Histologic diagnosis of dysplasia or early cancer should be confirmed by an expert pathologist.
Low-Grade Dysplasia (LGD)
  • Confirmation: Repeat upper endoscopy by an expert within 6 months on high-dose acid suppressive therapy to rule out prevalent HGD/EAC.
  • Management Choice: Shared decision-making between EET or continued surveillance.
  • Surveillance Plan (if chosen): Upper endoscopy at 6-month intervals for 1 year, then annually by an expert endoscopist.
Indefinite for Dysplasia (IND)
  • Progression Risk: Pooled incidence of HGD/EAC is 1.5 per 100 person-years.
  • Confirmation: Repeat endoscopy on BID PPI therapy within at least 3 months with repeat sampling.
  • Management: EET is not recommended for confirmed IND.
  • Surveillance Plan: Undergo annual endoscopy until there is a change in histologic grade.
Adjunctive Sampling: WATS-3D
  • Recommendation: The AGA makes no recommendation for or against the use of WATS-3D as an adjunctive sampling technique.
  • (Knowledge gap).
Evidence: Benefits (Incremental Yield)
  • HGD/EAC Detection: WATS-3D + Seattle protocol vs. Seattle alone showed an increased detection.
  • - Overall RR: 1.61 (95% CI 1.25-2.08).
  • - In screening/surveillance populations, the effect was attenuated and not statistically significant (RR 1.35).
  • Any Neoplasia Detection (LGD/HGD/EAC):
  • - Overall RR: 1.36 (95% CI 1.14-1.64).
Evidence: Harms & Concerns
  • No major adverse events reported.
  • Primary Concern: Downstream effects of potential false positive results, leading to unnecessary procedures or anxiety.
  • Longitudinal follow-up to confirm dysplasia found only on WATS-3D is limited.
Implementation Considerations
  • WATS-3D should not be used as a substitute for a structured biopsy protocol.
  • Discordant Results: If neoplasia is found on WATS-3D but not on biopsies, repeat endoscopy by an expert within 3-6 months.
  • Crypt Dysplasia, IND, or LGD on WATS-3D only: EET should not be performed.
Biomarkers for Risk Stratification
  • Current standard is histologic grade of dysplasia, but it has limitations (sampling error, interobserver variability).
  • This section evaluates commercially available biomarkers.
p53 Immunohistochemistry (IHC)
  • Recommendation: The AGA makes no recommendation for or against the routine use of p53 assessment.
  • (Knowledge gap).
Evidence: Predicting Progression
  • Aberrant p53 expression is associated with a significantly higher risk of progression to HGD/EAC.
  • - Overall RR vs. normal p53: 10.17 (95% CI 6.89-15).
  • - NDBE with aberrant p53: Progression rate of 7 per 100 person-years (vs. 0.5 without).
  • - LGD with aberrant p53: Progression rate of 11 per 100 person-years (vs. 1 without).
  • - IND with aberrant p53: Progression rate of 6 per 100 person-years (vs. 1 without).
Evidence: Harms & Test Performance
  • Overall Performance: Suboptimal diagnostic characteristics.
  • - Pooled Sensitivity: 0.48
  • - Pooled Specificity: 0.85
  • Major Concern: High number of false positives (FP).
  • - For NDBE, for every 3 true positives, there were 149 false positives.
  • - Calculated PPV was only 2% for 1 year of follow-up.
TissueCypher Testing
  • Recommendation: The AGA makes no recommendation for or against the routine use of TissueCypher testing.
  • (Knowledge gap).
Evidence: Predicting Progression (NDBE)
  • High-Risk vs. Intermediate/Low-Risk:
  • - Sensitivity: 37%, Specificity: 96%, OR: 13.55.
  • High/Intermediate-Risk vs. Low-Risk:
  • - Sensitivity: 52%, Specificity: 85%, OR: 6.0.
Evidence: Harms & Test Performance (NDBE)
  • Using the high/intermediate vs. low-risk cutoff:
  • - For every 3 true positives, there were 149 false positives.
  • - Calculated PPV was 2% and NPV was 99.6%.
  • Data for LGD and IND are very limited due to small sample sizes.
Chemoprevention Strategies
  • Focuses on medical and behavioral interventions to prevent neoplastic progression.
Proton Pump Inhibitor (PPI) Therapy
  • Recommendation: In patients with BE, the AGA suggests the use of daily PPI therapy compared to no PPI therapy for prevention of neoplastic progression.
  • (Conditional recommendation, low quality of evidence).
Evidence: Benefits of PPIs
  • Progression to HGD/EAC: Observational data shows a significant reduction in risk.
  • - Pooled OR: 0.47 (95% CI 0.32, 0.71).
  • - Absolute risk reduction: 84 fewer per 1,000.
  • Indirect Evidence (AspECT Trial): Compared high-dose (BID) vs. low-dose (daily) PPI. No significant difference in progression to HGD/EAC (RR 0.84).
Evidence: Harms of PPIs
  • RCT data (COMPASS trial) shows a small increased risk of enteric infections.
  • - OR for non-C. difficile enteric infections: 1.33 (95% CI 1.01, 1.75).
  • - Absolute risk: 4 more per 1,000.
  • No statistically significant increase in C. difficile, CKD, dementia, pneumonia, or fracture in RCTs.
Aspirin & High-Dose PPI
  • The AspECT trial showed a benefit for the combined endpoint of death, HGD, or EAC with high-dose PPI and aspirin.
  • However, there was no difference for the more relevant endpoint of HGD or EAC alone.
  • Therefore, no recommendation was made for aspirin or high-dose PPI therapy solely for chemoprevention.
Behavioral Modification
  • Tobacco Cessation: Smoking increases risk of BE progression (HR nearly double). Cessation should be encouraged.
  • Weight Loss: Central adiposity is a risk factor. Increasing BMI is associated with increased risk of progression. Weight loss is appropriate for overweight/obese patients.
Role of Anti-Reflux Surgery
  • Recommendation: In patients with BE, the AGA suggests the use of proton pump inhibitors over surgery for the prevention of neoplastic progression.
  • (Conditional recommendation, low quality of evidence).
Evidence: Benefits of Surgery vs. Medical Management
  • Progression to EAC: No significant difference found in pooled analysis.
  • - RR: 1.33 (95% CI 0.29, 6.16).
  • - The largest cohort study showed surgery did not reduce EAC risk (adjusted HR 1.9).
  • Progression to HGD/EAC: No significant difference found.
  • - RR: 0.86 (95% CI 0.30, 2.41).
Evidence: Harms of Anti-Reflux Surgery
  • Short-term Risks:
  • - 30-day all-cause mortality rate: 0.1%.
  • - Complication rate within 30 days: 4.1% (includes infection, bleeding, esophageal perforation).
  • Long-term Issues:
  • - Reflux recurrence: 17.7%.
  • - Need for secondary anti-reflux surgery: 16.4%.
  • - Failure rate at 5 years: 20-30%.
Discussion & Rationale
  • Existing evidence does not show consistent superiority of surgery over medical management for preventing progression.
  • Decision driven by the low risk of progression in NDBE, making it difficult to justify the risks associated with surgery.
Surveillance in Columnar Lined Esophagus <1 cm
  • Recommendation: In patients with columnar lined esophagus <1 cm with intestinal metaplasia without neoplasia, the AGA suggests against endoscopic surveillance.
  • (Conditional recommendation, very low certainty of evidence).
Evidence & Rationale
  • Progression Risk: The risk of progression to HGD/EAC is very low.
  • - Pooled incidence: 0.1 per 100 patient-years.
  • Diagnostic Uncertainty: The endoscopic diagnosis is fraught with substantial interobserver variability.
  • Resource Utilization: Life-long surveillance in this large population offers little benefit and incurs costs, risks, and patient anxiety.
Implementation Considerations
  • If the quality of the index exam is questionable, a one-time repeat high-quality exam can be performed to rule out prevalent neoplasia.
  • Shared decision-making is key; patients who place a high value on preventing EAC can reasonably choose a one-time repeat surveillance endoscopy.
Quality Indicators in BE Surveillance
  • Metrics to monitor and improve outcomes in patients with BE undergoing surveillance.
Established Quality Measures
  • Surveillance Intervals: Adhering to appropriate intervals (e.g., no sooner than 3-5 years in NDBE).
  • Sampling Technique: Appropriate use of the Seattle biopsy protocol.
  • - Studies show suboptimal adherence and wide variability for both measures.
Proposed Quality Metrics
  • Neoplasia Detection Rate (NDR): Prevalence of HGD or EAC on index screening endoscopy.
  • - A meta-analysis proposed a threshold of 4%.
  • Dysplasia Detection Rate (DDR): Prevalence of LGD or HGD during surveillance of NDBE.
Post-Endoscopy Esophageal Neoplasia (PEEN/PEEC)
  • Definition: HGD/EAC detected before the next recommended surveillance interval (between 6 months and 3 years after an index endoscopy negative for HGD/EAC).
  • Magnitude: A SR/MA reported that PEEC accounted for 21% of all EACs, and PEEN accounted for 26% of all HGD/EAC cases.
  • Implication: Suggests a high rate of missed neoplasia and an opportunity for quality improvement.
