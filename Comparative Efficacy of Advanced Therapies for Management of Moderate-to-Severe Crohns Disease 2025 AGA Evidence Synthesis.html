Comparative Efficacy of Advanced Therapies for Management of Moderate-to-Severe Crohn’s Disease: 2025 AGA Evidence Synthesis
  • - Journal: Gastroenterology
  • - Publication Year: 2025
  • - This Network Meta-Analysis (NMA) informs the 2025 update to the AGA Guidelines for moderate-to-severe Crohn's disease (CD).
Knowledge Test
  • - 1. In biologic-exposed patients, which two drug classes showed a likely important benefit over ustekinumab for maintenance therapy in treat-through trials?
  • - 2. Which drug class demonstrated significantly lower efficacy in patients with ileal CD compared to those with isolated colonic disease?
  • - 3. In the re-randomization maintenance trials, which drug and dose ranked highest for maintaining clinical remission among initial responders?
  • - 4. What was the pooled placebo rate for inducing clinical remission in biologic-naïve patients?
  • - 5. What key structural feature is absent in certolizumab pegol compared to infliximab and adalimumab, potentially affecting its mechanism of action?

Background & Study Rationale
  • - Crohn's disease (CD) affects over 1 million individuals in the U.S.
  • - Approximately 50% of patients require hospitalization or surgery.
  • - A growing number of advanced therapies (5 mechanisms, 9 drugs) necessitates guidance on positioning and sequencing.
  • - Head-to-head trials are sparse, making network meta-analyses (NMAs) crucial for comparing efficacy.

Methods & Key Definitions
  • - Population: Adults with moderately-to-severely active CD.
  • - Definition of Active Disease: Crohn’s Disease Activity Index (CDAI) score of 220–450.
Key Outcomes Measured
  • - Critical Outcome: Induction & Maintenance of Clinical Remission.
  • - Definition: CDAI score <150.
  • - Important Outcome: Induction & Maintenance of Clinical Response.
  • - Definition: 100-point decrease in CDAI from baseline.

Clinically Meaningful Difference (CMD)
  • - A difference between two agents was considered 'important' if the absolute risk difference was >50 per 1000 patients treated (5%).
  • - Differences between 0 and 5% were considered 'trivial'.

Assumed Placebo Rates
  • - Calculated by meta-analysis of placebo arms from included trials.
  • - Biologic-Naïve Patients: 19% for induction of clinical remission.
  • - Biologic-Exposed Patients: 14% for induction of clinical remission.

Maintenance Trial Designs
  • - Treat-through trials: Patients randomized to active or placebo and followed through induction and maintenance.
  • - Responder re-randomization trials: Only patients responding to induction are re-randomized for the maintenance phase.


Induction Therapy Efficacy
  • - Analysis of 28 trials for induction therapy.
  • - Stratified by prior biologic exposure status.
Biologic-Naïve Patients
  • - Pooled placebo rate for clinical remission: 19%.
Clinical Remission
  • - Most advanced therapies are effective compared to no treatment.
Efficacy vs. Placebo (Direct Evidence)
  • - Moderate-to-high certainty evidence for: infliximab, adalimumab, vedolizumab, ustekinumab, risankizumab, mirikizumab, guselkumab.
  • - Low certainty evidence for: certolizumab pegol, upadacitinib.

Comparative Efficacy (Active Comparators)
  • - Adalimumab & Ustekinumab vs. Certolizumab pegol:
  • - Showed an important benefit (moderate-to-high certainty).
  • - Adalimumab & Ustekinumab vs. Upadacitinib:
  • - Showed a likely important benefit (moderate certainty).
  • - Vedolizumab, Risankizumab, Mirikizumab, Guselkumab vs. Certolizumab pegol:
  • - Showed a possible important benefit (low certainty).

Drug Ranking (P-score)
  • - 1. Adalimumab (P-score: 0.83)
  • - 2. Ustekinumab (P-score: 0.70)

Anticipated Remission Rates (at 19% placebo rate)
  • - Adalimumab: 48%
  • - Ustekinumab / Vedolizumab: 43%
  • - Infliximab: 42%
  • - Guselkumab: 41%
  • - Mirikizumab: 40%
  • - Risankizumab: 38%


Clinical Response
  • - Findings were broadly consistent with clinical remission data.
  • - Top ranked agents: Infliximab (P-score: 0.98), Adalimumab (P-score: 0.78), Ustekinumab (P-score: 0.75).


Biologic-Exposed Patients
  • - Pooled placebo rate for clinical remission: 14%.
  • - Prior exposure was predominantly to TNF antagonists.
Clinical Remission
  • - Efficacy varies more significantly in this population.
Efficacy vs. Placebo (Direct Evidence)
  • - Moderate-to-high certainty evidence for: adalimumab, ustekinumab, risankizumab, guselkumab, upadacitinib.
  • - Low certainty evidence for: vedolizumab, mirikizumab.
Note on Adalimumab Data
  • - Trial only included patients with prior intolerance or secondary loss of response to infliximab.
  • - Patients with primary nonresponse to infliximab were excluded.


Comparative Efficacy (Active Comparators)
  • - Adalimumab, Risankizumab, Guselkumab vs. Vedolizumab:
  • - Likely important benefit (moderate certainty).
  • - Risankizumab & Guselkumab vs. Ustekinumab:
  • - Likely important benefit (moderate certainty).
  • - Adalimumab, Risankizumab, Upadacitinib vs. Mirikizumab:
  • - Possible important benefit (low certainty).

Drug Ranking (P-score)
  • - 1. Adalimumab (P-score: 0.87)
  • - 2. Guselkumab (P-score: 0.82)
  • - 3. Risankizumab (P-score: 0.71)

Anticipated Remission Rates (at 14% placebo rate)
  • - Adalimumab: 42%
  • - Guselkumab: 36%
  • - Risankizumab: 33%
  • - Upadacitinib: 30%
  • - Ustekinumab: 27%


Clinical Response
  • - Findings consistent with remission data.
  • - Top ranked agents: Guselkumab (P-score: 0.95) and Risankizumab (P-score: 0.81).



Maintenance Therapy Efficacy
  • - Analysis of 22 trials for maintenance therapy.
  • - Analyzed separately by trial design.
Treat-Through Design (at 1 year)
  • - Patients randomized at baseline and followed for 48-52 weeks.
Biologic-Naïve Patients
  • - The benefit of any specific intervention over another was uncertain.
  • - Top ranked agents: Guselkumab 200mg q4w (P-score: 0.74), Mirikizumab (P-score: 0.72), Ustekinumab (P-score: 0.68).

Biologic-Exposed Patients
  • - Risankizumab & Guselkumab ---> important benefit vs. Ustekinumab (moderate-to-high certainty).
  • - Mirikizumab ---> possible benefit vs. Ustekinumab (low certainty).
  • - Top ranked agents: Risankizumab (P-score: 0.96), Guselkumab 200mg q4w (P-score: 0.74).


Re-randomization of Responders
  • - Only patients who responded to induction therapy were included for maintenance analysis.
Key Comparisons
  • - Adalimumab showed higher rates of remission vs. Vedolizumab and Risankizumab (moderate-to-high certainty).
  • - Upadacitinib 30mg daily showed higher rates of remission vs. Certolizumab pegol, Vedolizumab, Ustekinumab, and Risankizumab (moderate-to-high certainty).

Drug Ranking (P-score)
  • - 1. Upadacitinib 30mg daily (P-score: 0.94)
  • - 2. Adalimumab (P-score: 0.85)



Safety Endpoints
  • - Regulatory trials are generally not powered to detect significant differences in safety.
Serious Adverse Events (SAEs)
  • - In maintenance trials, risk of SAEs was lower with Guselkumab 200mg q4w compared with:
  • - Infliximab
  • - Vedolizumab
  • - Ustekinumab
  • - Guselkumab 100mg q8w

Serious Infections
  • - Risk of serious infections was comparable between all active interventions and placebo.


Discussion & Key Clinical Implications
  • - This NMA provides crucial insights for positioning therapies in moderate-to-severe CD.
Impact of Prior Biologic Exposure
  • - Prior treatment exposure is a key determinant of subsequent therapy selection.
Lymphocyte Trafficking Inhibitors (Vedolizumab)
  • - May be more efficacious in biologic-naïve patients.

JAK Inhibitors (Upadacitinib)
  • - May be more efficacious in patients with prior TNF antagonist exposure.

IL-23p19 Antagonists (Risankizumab, Guselkumab)
  • - More effective than Ustekinumab (IL-12/23) primarily in patients with prior TNF antagonist exposure.
Biological Rationale
  • - Chronic inflammation in later-stage disease may be more dependent on IL-23 than IL-12.
  • - T-cells from TNF-refractory patients show up-regulation of the IL-23 receptor.



Impact of Disease Location
  • - Efficacy of some therapies differs based on the location of inflammation.
JAK Inhibitors (Upadacitinib)
  • - Showed strikingly lower efficacy in patients with ileal CD.
  • - Pivotal trials showed no significant efficacy signal in ileal disease.
  • - A separate meta-analysis confirmed efficacy was observed only in patients with isolated colonic disease (OR 4.37) but not ileal disease (OR 1.01).

Other Agents
  • - IL-23p19 and IL-12/23p40 antagonists demonstrated efficacy in both colonic (OR 4.29) and ileal disease (OR 2.31).
  • - Lymphocyte trafficking inhibitors had comparable, though low, efficacy in both colonic (OR 1.79) and ileal disease (OR 2.01).


Within-Class Differences in Efficacy
  • - Efficacy can vary even among drugs with the same mechanism of action.
TNF Antagonists
  • - Adalimumab was more effective than Certolizumab pegol in biologic-naïve patients.
  • - Potential Reason: Certolizumab pegol lacks the IgG1-Fc portion, preventing complement-mediated and antibody-mediated cell-dependent cytotoxicity.

IL-23p19 Antagonists
  • - Risankizumab may be more effective than Mirikizumab for inducing remission in biologic-exposed patients.


Limitations & Incoherence
  • - Dosing: Analysis used FDA-approved doses; real-world dose optimization may alter effectiveness.
  • - Prior Exposure Complexity: 'Biologic-exposed' is becoming more nuanced than just TNF-failure.
  • - Outcomes: Focused on clinical remission, not endoscopic outcomes, which were not consistently reported in older trials.
Statistical Incoherence Example
  • - For Risankizumab vs. Ustekinumab in biologic-exposed patients:
  • - Direct evidence (SEQUENCE trial) showed benefit: RR 1.47.
  • - Indirect evidence suggested no benefit: RR 0.82.
  • - The analysis relied on the head-to-head trial evidence in this case.
