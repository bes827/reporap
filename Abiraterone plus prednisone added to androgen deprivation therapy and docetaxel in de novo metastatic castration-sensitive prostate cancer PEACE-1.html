Abiraterone plus prednisone added to androgen deprivation therapy and docetaxel in de novo metastatic castration-sensitive prostate cancer (PEACE-1)
  • - Journal: The Lancet
  • - Publication Date: April 30, 2022
  • - Study Type: Phase 3, multicentre, open-label, randomised trial with a 2x2 factorial design.
Knowledge Test
  • - What was the specific dose of prednisone used in the PEACE-1 trial, and how does it compare to the dose used for metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer (mCRPC)?
  • - In the ADT with docetaxel population, what was the median CRPC-free survival for the group receiving abiraterone versus the group that did not?
  • - What percentage of patients in the control group (SOC without abiraterone) eventually received a next-generation hormonal therapy after progression?
  • - What was the minimum PSA value used to define mCRPC in this trial, even without radiographic progression?
  • - Besides hypertension, which other grade ≥3 adverse event was notably more frequent in the abiraterone group compared to the control group in the ADT + docetaxel population?

Background & Rationale
  • - Treatment for de novo metastatic castration-sensitive prostate cancer (mCSPC) has evolved beyond Androgen Deprivation Therapy (ADT) alone.
  • - Current standards of care (SOC) involve adding docetaxel, a second-generation androgen receptor axis inhibitor (e.g., abiraterone, apalutamide, enzalutamide), or radiotherapy to ADT.
  • - The key unanswered question was whether combining these new therapies (i.e., triplet therapy) could provide further clinical benefits.

PEACE-1 Study Design
  • - A multicentre, open-label, randomised, phase 3 study with a 2x2 factorial design.
  • - Aimed to evaluate the efficacy and safety of adding abiraterone and/or radiotherapy to the Standard of Care (SOC).
Patient Population
  • - 1173 male patients with de novo metastatic prostate adenocarcinoma.
  • - ECOG performance status of 0–1 (or 2 if due to bone pain).
  • - ADT initiated for no more than 3 months before randomisation.

Randomization & Arms (1:1:1:1)
  • - Arm 1: Standard of Care (SOC).
  • - Arm 2: SOC + Radiotherapy.
  • - Arm 3: SOC + Abiraterone.
  • - Arm 4: SOC + Radiotherapy + Abiraterone.
Evolution of Standard of Care (SOC) During Trial
  • - Initially (2013): SOC was ADT alone.
  • - Amendment 1 (Oct 2015): SOC allowed for ADT + docetaxel after CHAARTED/STAMPEDE results.
  • - Amendment 2 (Aug 2017): SOC mandated ADT + docetaxel for all new patients after LATITUDE/STAMPEDE results for abiraterone.


Treatment Regimens
  • - ADT: Continuous (GnRH agonist/antagonist or orchiectomy).
  • - Docetaxel: 75 mg/m² IV every 3 weeks for 6 cycles.
  • - Abiraterone: 1000 mg orally once daily.
  • - Prednisone: 5 mg orally twice daily (total 10mg/day, similar to the dose for mCRPC).

Endpoints
  • - Co-primary endpoints:
  • - Radiographic Progression-Free Survival (rPFS).
  • - Overall Survival (OS).
  • - Key secondary endpoint: Castration-Resistant Prostate Cancer (CRPC)-free survival.


Efficacy Results
  • - Median follow-up for OS was 4.4 years.
  • - No significant interaction was found between abiraterone and radiotherapy, allowing for a pooled analysis of abiraterone's effect.
Overall Population (ADT +/- Docetaxel)
  • - Compares all patients receiving abiraterone (n=583) vs. all patients not receiving abiraterone (n=589).
Radiographic Progression-Free Survival (rPFS)
  • - Median rPFS improved from 2.2 years to 4.5 years.
  • - 46% risk reduction of progression or death.
  • - HR: 0.54 (99.9% CI 0.41–0.71; p<0.0001).

Overall Survival (OS)
  • - Median OS improved from 4.7 years to 5.7 years.
  • - 18% risk reduction of death.
  • - HR: 0.82 (95.1% CI 0.69–0.98; p=0.030).


Population of Interest: ADT + Docetaxel (Triplet Therapy)
  • - This is the key comparison for establishing triplet therapy as a new SOC (n=355 with abiraterone vs. n=355 without).
Radiographic Progression-Free Survival (rPFS)
  • - Median rPFS more than doubled from 2.0 years to 4.5 years.
  • - 50% risk reduction of progression or death.
  • - HR: 0.50 (99.9% CI 0.34–0.71; p<0.0001).

Overall Survival (OS)
  • - Median OS: Not reached vs. 4.4 years.
  • - 25% risk reduction of death.
  • - HR: 0.75 (95.1% CI 0.59–0.95; p=0.017).

Subgroup Analysis by Disease Burden
  • - High Burden Definition: Visceral metastases or ≥4 bone lesions with ≥1 outside the vertebral bodies/pelvis.
High-Volume Disease
  • - Clear OS benefit with triplet therapy.
  • - Median OS improved from 3.5 years to 5.1 years.
  • - 28% risk reduction of death (HR 0.72, p=0.019).

Low-Volume Disease
  • - No conclusive OS benefit yet (data immature).
  • - HR for OS: 0.83 (p=0.66).
  • - However, a major benefit in rPFS was observed (HR 0.58).


Other Key Endpoints
  • - CRPC-free survival: Median improved from 1.45 years to 3.21 years (HR 0.38).
  • - Prostate-cancer-specific survival: Improved with abiraterone (HR 0.69).



Safety & Toxicity (ADT + Docetaxel Population)
  • - Adding abiraterone to ADT+Docetaxel resulted in a modest increase in toxicity.
  • - Grade ≥3 AEs: 63% with abiraterone vs. 52% without.
  • - Fatal (Grade 5) AEs: 2% with abiraterone vs. 1% without.
Specific Grade ≥3 Adverse Events (Abiraterone vs. Control)
  • - Hypertension: 22% vs. 13% (largest difference).
  • - Hepatotoxicity (aminotransferase increase): 6% vs. 1%.
  • - Febrile Neutropenia: 5% vs. 5% (no increase).
  • - Neutropenia: 10% vs. 9% (no increase).
  • - Fatigue: 3% vs. 4% (no increase).
  • - Peripheral Neuropathy: 1% vs. 2% (no increase).

Note on Drug Interactions
  • - No evidence of increased chemotherapy-related side effects.
  • - Suggests no significant drug-drug interaction between abiraterone and docetaxel affecting toxicity.


Discussion & Clinical Implications
  • - PEACE-1 is the first trial to show an OS benefit for a triplet systemic therapy (ADT + Docetaxel + Abiraterone) in de novo mCSPC.
New Standard of Care
  • - Triplet therapy should be considered a new SOC for patients with high-volume mCSPC who are fit for docetaxel.
  • - For low-volume disease, the decision is less clear due to immature OS data, requiring shared decision-making.

Upfront Combination vs. Sequential Therapy
  • - Benefit was seen even though over 80% of the control group received a next-generation hormonal therapy upon progression.
  • - This suggests upfront combination is superior to sequential administration of these agents.
Definition of Progression in Control Arm
  • - Investigators were allowed to start salvage therapy early.
  • - CRPC was defined by radiographic progression or a confirmed PSA rise with a minimum value of just 0.50 ng/mL and castrate testosterone.


Limitations & Unanswered Questions
  • - Open-label design.
  • - Does not answer if triplet therapy is superior to ADT + abiraterone alone.
  • - Unclear if findings apply to metachronous (relapsing) mCSPC, as only de novo patients were included.


Conclusion
  • - The triplet combination of ADT, docetaxel, and abiraterone improved both overall survival and radiographic progression-free survival in men with de novo mCSPC.
  • - This triplet therapy could become a new standard of care, especially for patients with high-volume disease.
  • - The toxicity profile was manageable, with a modest increase primarily in hypertension.
