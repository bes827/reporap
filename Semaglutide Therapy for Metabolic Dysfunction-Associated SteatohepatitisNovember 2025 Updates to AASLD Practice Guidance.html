FDA Approval & Indication (Wegovy®)
  • - Drug: Semaglutide (Wegovy® formulation).
  • - Approval: Accelerated FDA approval on August 15, 2025.
  • - Indication: Treatment of MASH with moderate-to-advanced fibrosis (consistent with stages F2-F3).
Basis for Approval: ESSENCE Trial (Phase 3)
  • - Based on interim results from the first 800 participants.
  • - Final approval is contingent on long-term outcomes, expected in 2028 or later.
Primary Histologic Endpoints (at 72 weeks)
  • - Dose: 2.4 mg/week subcutaneous injection.
  • - Both primary endpoints were achieved.
MASH Resolution
  • - Resolution of MASH without worsening of fibrosis.
  • - Achieved in 62.9% of patients vs. 34.3% with placebo (p<0.001).

Fibrosis Improvement
  • - >1 stage reduction in liver fibrosis without worsening of MASH.
  • - Achieved in 36.8% of patients vs. 22.4% with placebo (p<0.001).



Other Approved Indications for Semaglutide
  • - Chronic Weight Management (2021): Adults with obesity or overweight with at least one weight-related comorbidity.
  • - Pediatric Weight Management (2022): Patients aged 12 years and older.
  • - Cardiovascular Risk Reduction (2024): Adults with established CV disease and either obesity or overweight.


Patient Selection: Whom to Treat
  • - Key candidates: Patients with MASH and fibrosis stage F2-F3.
  • - Methodology: Identification should be based on Non-Invasive Tests (NITs) rather than liver biopsy, which is deemed impractical for most patients.
Recommended NIT Cutoffs for Treatment Eligibility
  • - These ranges are recommended to identify patients with F2-F3 fibrosis.
VCTE (Vibration Controlled Transient Elastography)
  • - Recommended LSM range: 8–15 kPa.
  • - This range included approximately 50% of the ESSENCE study population.

MRE (Magnetic Resonance Elastography)
  • - Recommended LSM range: 3.1–4.4 kPa.
  • - MRE was not used in ESSENCE, but criteria are extrapolated from prior studies.

ELF™ (Enhanced Liver Fibrosis) Score
  • - Recommended range: 9.2–10.5.
  • - This range identified approximately 50% of participants in ESSENCE with histological F2-3.


Individualized Decision Zone (Higher NIT values)
  • - For patients with NIT values in these higher ranges, an individualized decision to treat is needed after excluding cirrhosis.
NIT Ranges
  • - VCTE: 15-20 kPa
  • - MRE: 4.4-5.0 kPa
  • - ELF: 10.5-11.3

Exclusion of Cirrhosis
  • - Use a confirmatory NIT.
  • - Use cross-sectional imaging to exclude nodular liver contour or signs of portal hypertension.
  • - Check for platelet count < 150,000/mm3.


Populations Not Recommended for MASH Indication
  • - MASH Cirrhosis: Not approved for this indication.
  • - Defined by VCTE > 20 kPa, MRE > 5.0 kPa, or ELF > 11.3.
  • - Note: Patients with compensated cirrhosis receiving semaglutide for another approved indication should be monitored carefully.

Role of FIB-4
  • - A FIB-4 score < 1.3 effectively excludes advanced fibrosis (F3+).
  • - A FIB-4 score ≥ 1.3 has a weak positive predictive value.
  • - Should not be used as a single rule-in or rule-out test; AASLD recommends a sequential testing strategy.

Consideration of Alcohol Use
  • - ESSENCE trial allowed mild alcohol use (up to 20 g/d in women / 30 g/d in men).
  • - Alcohol consumption should be assessed with AUDIT-C and PEth testing.
  • - Decisions to treat patients with moderate alcohol consumption must be individualized.


Monitoring During Treatment
  • - Monitoring parameters are similar to other Wegovy® indications.
Hepatic Function Monitoring
  • - In the ESSENCE trial, hepatic function was tested every 12 weeks.
  • - Result: None of the 538 participants stopped treatment due to elevated ALT or AST.
  • - Recommendation: Hepatic function panels should be performed as clinically indicated, not on a strict schedule.

Adverse Event Monitoring
  • - Monitor for common GI adverse events (nausea, diarrhea, constipation, vomiting).
  • - Screen for active suicidal ideation; if present, do not initiate until stable and under care of a mental health professional.


Adverse Events & Safety Monitoring
  • - Overall adverse event rate: 86.2% with semaglutide vs. 79.7% with placebo.
  • - Discontinuation rates due to adverse events were similar: 2.6% (semaglutide) vs. 3.3% (placebo).
Gastrointestinal (GI) Disorders (Most Common)
  • - Most GI side effects were mild/moderate and transient.
Incidence vs. Placebo
  • - Nausea: 36.2% vs. 13.2%
  • - Diarrhea: 26.9% vs. 12.2%
  • - Constipation: 22.2% vs. 8.4%
  • - Vomiting: 18.6% vs. 5.6%

Management Strategies
  • - Patient Education: Stop eating when feeling full to avoid nausea/bloating.
  • - Dose Titration: Gradual increase in dose.
  • - Dietary: Smaller, lower-fat meals, adequate hydration, dietary fiber.
  • - Supportive Meds: Antiemetics, prokinetics, or antidiarrheals if needed.


Key Safety Considerations (See Table 3)
  • - A summary of rare but serious risks requiring clinical awareness.
Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)
  • - Cause: Volume depletion from GI side effects.
  • - Action: Ensure hydration, monitor serum creatinine/eGFR, especially during dose titration.

Acute Pancreatitis
  • - Action: Discontinue promptly if suspected. Avoid re-initiation after a confirmed episode.

Gallbladder Disease
  • - Risk: Significantly increased risk. A meta-analysis showed a 37% increased risk of gallbladder/biliary diseases.
  • - Incidence: Cholelithiasis (1.6% vs 0.7%), Cholecystitis (0.6% vs 0.2%).
  • - Action: Educate patients on symptoms; use caution in those with a history of gallbladder disease.

Thyroid C-cell Tumors
  • - Warning: Black box warning for risk of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC).
  • - Contraindication: Personal or family history of MTC or Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN2).

Hypoglycemia
  • - Risk: Low with monotherapy, but increases when used with insulin or sulfonylureas.

Heart Rate Increase
  • - Mechanism: Direct chronotropic effect on the sinoatrial node.
  • - Action: Instruct patients to report palpitations or a racing heartbeat at rest.

Gastroparesis
  • - Risk: Can precipitate gastroparesis in rare cases.
  • - Contraindication: Use in severe gastroparesis is contraindicated.

Diabetic Retinopathy
  • - Risk: May increase complications, especially during rapid glycemic improvement.
  • - SUSTAIN-6 Trial: Complications occurred in 3.0% of semaglutide patients vs. 1.8% in placebo.

Lean Mass Loss / Sarcopenia
  • - Lean mass loss constitutes about 39% of total weight reduction.
  • - Appendicular skeletal muscle volume declines by 9–10% over two years.
Mitigation Strategies
  • - Protein Intake: Ensure adequate intake of 1.2–1.5 g/kg/day.
  • - Exercise: Encourage resistance training.
  • - Monitoring: Closely monitor older or sarcopenic adults.




Assessing Response to Treatment
  • - No single NIT reliably predicts histologic response at the individual level.
  • - The following are suggested markers of a beneficial response at 72 weeks.
Criteria for Beneficial Response
  • - These thresholds suggest a significant improvement and likely reflect a beneficial response.
Fibrosis Improvement Markers
  • - VCTE LSM: Decrease ≥ 30%
  • - MRE LSM: Decrease ≥ 20%
  • - ELF Score: Decrease ≥ 0.5

MASH Resolution Markers
  • - ALT: Decrease > 17 U/L or ≥ 20%
  • - CAP: Decrease > 30%


Assessing Futility (Non-Response)
  • - In ESSENCE, 37.1% of participants did not resolve MASH, and 63.2% did not have fibrosis reduction by Week 72.
  • - Suspect non-response if:
  • - - Lack of decline in ALT or liver stiffness.
  • - - Worsening of NIT parameters.


Concomitant Therapy
  • - Lifestyle Modification: Remains the cornerstone of MASLD management, including nutrition, exercise, and behavior modification.
Combination with Resmetirom
  • - The combination of semaglutide 2.4 mg/week and resmetirom has not been studied.
  • - In the MAESTRO-NASH trial, 14% of participants were on a GLP-1 RA and showed similar rates of MASH resolution and fibrosis improvement.

Other Therapies
  • - Vitamin E (≥800 IU/day) & Pioglitazone: Patients were excluded from ESSENCE if these were not at a stable dose for 90 days prior to baseline.


Semaglutide & Cardiometabolic Risk Factors
  • - Cardiovascular disease is the most common cause of death in patients with MASH.
Obesity (STEP Trials)
  • - MASLD guidance recommends >10% weight loss to reverse fibrosis.
  • - STEP 1: Average weight loss of 14.9%.
  • - STEP 2 (with T2DM): Average weight loss of 9.6%.
  • - STEP 3 (with behavioral therapy): Average weight loss of 16%.
  • - Discontinuation leads to weight regain and reversal of cardiometabolic benefits.

Cardiovascular Risk Reduction
  • - SUSTAIN-6 Trial (Ozempic® in T2DM): Showed a 2.3% absolute risk reduction in major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE).
  • - SELECT Trial (Wegovy® in Obesity without Diabetes): Demonstrated a 1.5% absolute risk reduction in MACE over ~40 months.

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) (FLOW Trial)
  • - For patients with T2DM and CKD, semaglutide 1.0 mg weekly showed a 24% relative risk reduction in a composite of kidney failure, CV death, and other renal events.


Endocrinologic Considerations & Mechanism of Action
  • - Semaglutide is a GLP-1 Receptor Agonist with 94% sequence homology to human GLP-1.
  • - It has a long half-life of approximately 5-7 days, allowing for once-weekly dosing.
Hepatic Effects (Indirect)
  • - GLP-1 receptor expression is negligible on hepatocytes and other resident liver cells.
  • - Hepatoprotective benefits are mediated indirectly via:
  • - - Sustained weight reduction.
  • - - Improved glycemic and lipid profiles.
  • - - Reduction of hepatic and systemic inflammation.

Systemic Effects
  • - Pancreas: Stimulates glucose-dependent insulin release and suppresses glucagon release.
  • - Central Nervous System: Mediates appetite suppression by activating GLP-1Rs in the hypothalamus and hindbrain.
  • - Lipid Metabolism: Inhibits chylomicron synthesis, decreases de novo lipogenesis, and reduces VLDL production.


Summary of Recommendations
  • - This section provides a condensed overview of the key guidance points.
FDA Indication
  • - Semaglutide (Wegovy®) is approved for MASH with F2-F3 fibrosis based on ESSENCE trial interim results showing significant MASH resolution and fibrosis improvement.

Patient Selection
  • - Use NITs to identify candidates: VCTE (8–15 kPa), MRE (3.1–4.4 kPa), or ELF (9.2–10.5).
  • - Avoid routine liver biopsy.
  • - Not approved for MASH cirrhosis.

Monitoring & Safety
  • - Favorable hepatic safety profile; monitor liver enzymes only as clinically indicated.
  • - Manage common GI side effects with patient education and dose titration.
  • - Be aware of rare but serious risks like pancreatitis, gallbladder disease, and lean mass loss.

Treatment Response
  • - Lifestyle modification is essential.
  • - Response can be suggested by significant reductions in NITs (e.g., VCTE LSM >30%, MRE LSM >20%, ALT >20%).
  • - Suspect non-response if NITs worsen.
