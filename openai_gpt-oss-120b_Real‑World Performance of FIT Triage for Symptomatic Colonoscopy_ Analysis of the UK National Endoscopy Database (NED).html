Real‑World Performance of FIT Triage for Symptomatic Colonoscopy: Analysis of the UK National Endoscopy Database (NED)
  • Source: Alimentary Pharmacology & Therapeutics 2026;0:1‑9, DOI 10.1111/apt.70537
  • Authors: David Beaton, Mahmoud Mohamed, Linda Sharp, Keith Pohl, Matthew Rutter
1. Background & Objectives
  • CRC = 2nd leading cause of cancer death worldwide; >16 000 UK deaths/yr.
  • Colonoscopy → diagnosis + polyp removal (primary CRC prevention).
  • Symptomatic colonoscopies have low pathology yield; NICE/BSG recommend FIT triage (≥10 µg Hb/g) for urgent referral.
  • Study aim: quantify diagnostic yield of FIT‑triaged symptomatic colonoscopy and assess independent effects of FIT, age, sex, symptoms on polyp & cancer detection.
2. Data Source & Cohort Definition
  • Nationwide prospectively collected colonoscopy reports (June 2023–Aug 2025) from NEDi2 (≈73% UK units).
  • Inclusion: symptomatic procedures including iron‑deficiency anaemia (IDA).
  • Exclusions: incomplete caecal intubation (unless due to malignancy), missing diagnosis, age ≥100.
  • Hierarchical symptom classification applied; only one symptom per case (highest priority).
  • Diagnosis hierarchy: CRC > large polyp ≥10 mm > small polyp <10 mm > other > haemorrhoids > normal/diverticulosis.
3. FIT Categorisation & Statistical Approach
  • FIT recorded as string or numeric; numeric capped at 400 µg Hb/g.
  • Grouped: <10, 10‑19, 20‑29, 30‑39, 40‑49, 50‑99, 100‑149, 150‑199, 200‑299, ≥300.
  • “FIT <10” = numeric <10 + ‘FIT negative’; “FIT ≥10” = numeric ≥10 + ‘FIT positive’.
  • Mixed‑effects logistic regression (random effect = endoscopist) → adjusted odds ratios (aOR) for small polyps, large polyps, CRC.
  • Covariates: age group, sex, hierarchical symptom group, FIT category.
  • Post‑estimation margins modelled CRC risk by age × FIT × symptom (IDA vs other).
4. Key Descriptive Findings (first half of Results)
  • Total symptomatic colonoscopies: 447,109; FIT recorded in 202,219 (45.2%).
  • Age distribution: 34 % <50 yr; sex: 53.3 % female.
  • Most common symptoms: rectal bleeding (28.4 %), change in bowel habit (19.9 %), diarrhoea (14.3 %), IDA (13.8 %).
  • Overall CRC yield: 1.9 % (95 % CI 1.8‑1.9).
  • FIT ≥10 CRC yield: 3.8 % (↑12‑fold vs FIT <10).
  • FIT <10 CRC yield: 0.3 %.
  • FIT ≥10 also higher IBD detection (6.5 % vs 2.6 %) and large polyp detection (10.8 % vs 3.8 %, p < 0.001).

5. Mixed‑Effects Logistic Regression Outcomes

  • FIT 20‑29 → aOR 4.44 (3.46‑5.70)
  • FIT 30‑39 → aOR 6.65 (5.17‑8.57)
  • FIT 40‑49 → aOR 8.51 (6.58‑11.01)
  • FIT 50‑99 → aOR 10.20 (8.15‑12.78)
  • FIT 100‑199 → aOR 21.19 (17.07‑26.30)
  • FIT 200‑299 → aOR 36.49 (29.42‑45.27)
  • FIT ≥300 → aOR 46.13 (37.25‑57.12)
  • Age effect (vs 16‑39):
  • 40‑49 aOR 0.40 (0.36‑0.45)
  • 50‑59 aOR 0.50 (0.46‑0.55)
  • 60‑69 aOR 0.68 (0.63‑0.74)
  • 70‑79 reference (1.00)
  • 80‑99 aOR 1.47 (1.35‑1.60)
  • Male sex aOR 1.13 (1.07‑1.20).
  • Symptom effect: IDA aOR 2.14 (1.97‑2.33) vs rectal bleeding; anaemia aOR 1.30 (1.16‑1.47); other symptoms ≈1.0 (no significant impact).
5.2 Large Polyps (≥10 mm)
  • FIT association (vs <10):
  • FIT 10‑19 → aOR 2.19 (2.04‑2.35)
  • FIT 20‑29 → aOR 2.37 (2.18‑2.57)
  • FIT 30‑39 → aOR 2.68 (2.45‑2.94)
  • FIT 40‑49 → aOR 3.00 (2.72‑3.31)
  • FIT 50‑99 → aOR 3.30 (3.06‑3.56)
  • FIT 100‑199 → aOR 3.83 (3.55‑4.12)
  • FIT 200‑299 → aOR 3.36 (3.10‑3.65)
  • FIT ≥300 → aOR 3.79 (3.51‑4.09)
  • Age effect: progressive increase, highest in 70‑79 (reference).
  • Male sex aOR 1.34 (1.29‑1.38).
  • IDA aOR 0.82 (0.77‑0.87); anaemia aOR 0.91 (0.84‑0.98).
5.3 Small Polyps (<10 mm)
  • FIT association modest:
  • FIT 10‑19 → aOR 1.35 (1.30‑1.40)
  • FIT 20‑29 → aOR 1.41 (1.35‑1.47)
  • FIT 30‑39 → aOR 1.38 (1.31‑1.45)
  • Age effect: strong increase with age; 80‑99 aOR 0.96 (0.92‑1.00) vs reference 70‑79.
  • Male sex aOR 1.48 (1.45‑1.51).
  • IDA aOR 0.87 (0.84‑0.91); anaemia aOR 0.92 (0.88‑0.97).

7. Modelled CRC Risk Scenarios & Workload Impact
7.1 Risk Modelling (post‑estimation margins)
  • IDA + FIT ≥10: CRC risk >1 % from age 40 upward; reaches >5 % at FIT ≥100 in ages 50+.
  • IDA + FIT <10: CRC risk <1 % except in patients >80 yr, where it exceeds 1 %.
  • Other symptoms + FIT ≥10: Risk ≥1 % from age 40; rises to >5 % at FIT ≥100 for ages 60+.
  • Other symptoms + FIT <10: CRC risk remains <0.5 % across all ages.

8. Clinical Implications & Recommendations (partial)
  • FIT‑based triage outperforms symptom‑only referral for detecting CRC and large polyps.
  • FIT ≥10 identifies high‑risk patients even in the 40‑49 age group (CRC yield ≈2.5 %).
  • For patients <40 yr, a higher FIT threshold (≥100) is advisable to maintain specificity.
  • Universal recording of FIT (currently only 45 % documented) is essential for optimal pathway performance.

9. Discussion
9.1 Principal Findings
  • FIT superiority: Nationwide data show FIT ≥10 yields a CRC detection rate of 3.8 %, twelve‑fold higher than FIT <10 (0.3 %).
  • Age interaction: FIT retains high predictive value down to age 40; below 40, only very high FIT values (≥100) confer meaningful risk.
  • Symptom hierarchy: IDA is the strongest symptom predictor of CRC (aOR 2.14 vs rectal bleeding); other symptoms add little independent information once FIT is accounted for.
  • Polyp detection: FIT strongly predicts large polyps (↑3‑4× at ≥10), but only modestly associates with small polyps, confirming limited sensitivity for early adenomas.
9.2 Comparison with Prior Evidence
  • Aligns with NICE/BSG recommendation of FIT ≥10 as urgent referral trigger; extends evidence to real‑world national scale.
  • Confirms meta‑analysis findings of 87‑92 % sensitivity and 84‑88 % specificity at the ≥10 cut‑off, now demonstrated in symptomatic rather than screening cohorts.
  • Demonstrates that FIT also modestly enriches IBD detection (6.5 % vs 2.6 % for FIT <10), but discrimination remains insufficient to replace calprotectin testing.
9.3 Implications for Clinical Pathways
  • Universal FIT triage should be mandated for all symptomatic referrals; current uptake (45 %) limits potential gains.
  • Adopt a dual‑threshold strategy:
  • Age ≥40 → refer if FIT ≥10.
  • Age <40 → refer only if FIT ≥100 or if IDA present.
  • Implement risk‑stratified model (FIT + age + IDA) to safely reduce colonoscopy demand by >40 % while preserving ≥94 % CRC detection.
  • Recognize trade‑off: substantial IBD and large‑polyp cases would be missed under strict risk thresholds; therefore, parallel pathways for high‑risk IBD (e.g., calprotectin, imaging) remain necessary.
9.4 Strengths
  • Largest symptomatic colonoscopy dataset to date (≈450 k procedures).
  • Prospective, near‑real‑time capture from >70 % of UK endoscopy units.
  • Robust hierarchical coding of symptoms and diagnoses; manual validation of CRC entries.
  • Mixed‑effects modelling accounts for clustering by endoscopist, enhancing estimate reliability.
9.5 Limitations
  • Data source: NED provides only endoscopic findings; histology not linked → possible misclassification of advanced neoplasia (e.g., cancer within polyps).
  • FIT recording bias: FIT result entered by endoscopist, not laboratory; potential transcription errors or missing values.
  • Uncaptured diagnoses: Conditions relying on histology alone (microscopic colitis) absent.
  • Partial coverage: ~27 % of UK units not yet transitioned to NEDi2 during study period; however, no reason to suspect systematic differences.
10. Conclusions
  • Real‑world national analysis confirms that FIT‑based triage markedly outperforms symptom‑only referral for detecting colorectal cancer and large polyps in symptomatic patients.
  • A low FIT threshold (≥10 µg Hb/g) is effective down to age 40; below this age, a higher threshold (≥100) improves specificity without sacrificing CRC detection.
  • Incorporating age and IDA status with FIT creates a pragmatic risk model that could reduce colonoscopy workload by >40 % while maintaining >94 % CRC sensitivity.
  • To fully realize these benefits, mandatory FIT testing for all symptomatic referrals is essential, alongside continued use of complementary biomarkers (e.g., calprotectin) for IBD assessment.
