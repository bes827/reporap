SUBMITTING THE APPLICATION AND THE GRANT REVIEW & AWARD PROCESS
  • Source: Writing the NIH Grant Proposal: A Step‑by‑Step Guide (2026)
1. Registration & System Setup
  • Register in eRA Commons, Grants.gov, and SAM before any submission.
  • Institutional registration for Grants.gov = one‑time; SAM = annual renewal.
  • Requires an Authorized Organization Representative (AOR) or Signing Official (SO) to complete registrations.
  • PI must have a separate account for PD/PI role; if PI = SO, create two distinct accounts.
  • Minimum 6‑week lead time for NIH to ingest new institutional registrations.
2. Proposal Preparation & FOA Alignment
  • Locate an active Funding Opportunity Announcement (FOA) matching research aims.
  • Verify grant‑type specific due dates; do not confuse Letter of Intent deadline with application deadline.
  • Confirm any required pre‑submission lead time set by the applicant’s office (often weeks before agency deadline).
  • Draft all components per FOA instructions; resolve conflicts between Application Guide and FOA → follow FOA.
  • Consult NIH FAQs for clarification; they do not override FOA but can aid interpretation.
3. Identifying Review Structure & Assignment Requests
  • Determine target Institute/Center (I/C) based on scientific focus.
  • Identify appropriate Integrated Review Group (IRG) and Study Section (SRG) via NIH IRG list.
  • Example: For blood‑pressure breathing study → Risk, Prevention, and Health Behavior (RPHB) IRG, Behavioral Medicine Interventions & Outcomes (BMIO) Study Section.
  • Review BMIO membership roster; assess reviewer expertise (e.g., heart‑rate variability).
  • Complete New Assignment Request Form: request I/C assignment, specific study section, list individuals to avoid, and specify needed expertise areas.
4. Cover Letter (Optional but Recommended)
  • Include application title, FOA name, and any late‑submission justification if applicable.
  • State reasons for changed/corrected application and attach prior cover letter text.
  • Disclose subaward budget components not active each period.
  • Attach required agency approvals (e.g., $500 k+ projects, conference grants).
  • Note intent to submit video supplement; otherwise video will be rejected.
  • Declare generation of large‑scale genomic data per NIH Genomic Data Sharing Policy.

End of Part 1 – covering registration through cover‑letter preparation.

5. Submission Process & Immediate Post‑Submission Checks
  • AOR submits the assembled application via Grants.gov (or institutional S2S portal).
  • After submission, NIH provides a 2‑business‑day validation window to view the assembled image in eRA Commons.
  • Only changes allowed up to FOA deadline; no “two‑day edit period” after that.
  • Verify status in eRA Commons → Application Information → “Submitted”.
6. Tracking & Confirmation of Receipt
  • Monitor eRA Commons for submission receipt, validation receipt, and agency tracking number.
  • Email notifications from Grants.gov are unreliable; rely on eRA Commons dashboard.
  • Ensure the proposal is viewable; if not, NIH cannot review it → contact grants office immediately.
7. Review Timeline & Cycle Overview
  • Review cycles (Table 8.1) repeat three times per year:
  • Cycle I: Due Jan 25–May 7 → CSR review Jun‑Jul → I/C/Advisory Council Aug → Project start Sep.
  • Cycle II: Due May 25–Sep 7 → CSR Oct‑Nov → Council Jan → Start Apr.
  • Cycle III: Due Sep 25–Jan 7 → CSR Feb‑Mar → Council May → Start Jul.
8. Level 1 Review – Center for Scientific Review (CSR)
8.1 Division of Receipt & Referral (DRR)
  • Checks FOA compliance, assigns Application ID, Scientific Review Officer (SRO), IRG, SRG, and target I/C.
  • ID encodes: Application Type, Activity Code, Institute Code, Serial Number, Support Year, Suffixes (e.g., 1 R01 HL 97865 01).
8.2 Scientific Review Officer (SRO) Actions
  • Selects ≥3 potential reviewers; at least two provide written critiques and lead discussion.
  • May form a Special Emphasis Panel (SEP) for conflicts or niche expertise.
  • Contacts PI if critical elements are missing; allows submission of supplemental materials (e.g., updated biosketch, letters of support) per SRG policy.
8.3 Study Section / Scientific Review Group (SRG) Meeting
  • Each SRG reviews up to ~100 applications; members receive packets ≈6 weeks before meeting.
  • Reviewers disclose conflicts of interest; conflicted members excluded from discussion.
  • Applications ranked top 50th percentile receive full discussion and priority scoring; others are streamlined, receiving written critiques only.
8.3.1 Outcomes of SRG Meeting
  • Discussed applications: Primary & secondary reviewers present, 10‑15 min group discussion, then private priority score (9‑point scale).
  • Scores converted to impact scores (average × 10 → range 10–90).
  • Non‑discussed applications: Marked “Not Recommended for Further Consideration (NRFC)”, “Deferred”, or “Streamlined”.
8.4 Post‑Meeting Deliverables
  • Priority scores & percentiles posted to eRA Commons within days.
  • Summary Statement (formerly “pink sheet”) uploaded ≈1 month later; contains reviewer critiques, overall impact score, percentile, budget recommendation, and any human/animal subject codes.
9. Level 2 Review – Institute/Center (I/C) Advisory Council
9.1 Transmission of Materials
  • Summary statements and scores transmitted to the target I/C for final funding consideration.
9.2 Advisory Council / Board Evaluation
  • Council members (extramural scientists + public reps) assess:
  • Score & percentile relative to I/C payline.
  • Relevance to institute mission and alignment with strategic priorities.
  • Compliance issues (human subjects, animal welfare, biohazards).
9.3 Funding Decisions & Just‑In‑Time (JIT) Requests
  • Applications within payline may receive a JIT request for:
  • Updated Other Support, Human Subjects Assurance, IRB approval, Animal Welfare Assurance, etc.
  • JIT does not guarantee funding; final decision rests with the I/C Director after Council recommendation.
9.4 Final Outcomes
  • Council can issue one of three decisions: Recommended for Funding, Not Recommended, or Deferred back to SRG.
  • Upon approval, a Notice of Award (NoA) is generated; award terms negotiated thereafter.
10. Scoring System & Interpretation
10.1 Impact/Priority Score Scale (Table 8.3)
  • 1 Exceptional → no weaknesses.
  • 2 Outstanding → negligible weaknesses.
  • 3 Excellent → minor weaknesses only.
  • 4 Very Good → numerous minor weaknesses.
  • 5 Good → at least one moderate weakness.
  • 6 Satisfactory → some moderate weaknesses.
  • 7 Fair → at least one major weakness.
  • 8 Marginal → several major weaknesses.
  • 9 Poor → many major weaknesses.
10.2 Percentile Rank
  • Applies to research (non‑K) applications reviewed by CSR.
  • Percentile = % of applications scoring better over the last three SRG meetings.
  • Used to counter “priority score creep” and provide a relative performance metric.
10.3 Summary Statement Content
  • Bullet‑point critiques for each of Five Review Criteria (Significance, Investigators, Innovation, Approach, Environment).
  • Includes: Priority/Impact Score, Percentile, Recommended Budget, Human/Animal Subject Codes, and Administrative Comments.
11. Post‑Review Actions & Resubmission Guidance
  • If NRFC, Deferred, or score below payline, consider revision and resubmission (new FOA cycle).
  • Address reviewer‑identified weaknesses; update Specific Aims, Approach, or Preliminary Data as needed.
  • For JIT requests, gather required documentation promptly; upload via eRA Commons once official email received.

End of Part 2 – covering submission, tracking, CSR and I/C review, scoring, and post‑review actions.

12. Common Pitfalls & Troubleshooting
  • Late registration → NIH cannot process; start ≥6 weeks before submission.
  • Missing AOR/SO signature → application rejected at validation stage.
  • Incorrect FOA selection → mismatched due dates, may lead to ineligible submission.
  • Failure to upload required attachments (e.g., biosketch, budget) → “incomplete” status, no review.
  • Conflicts of interest not disclosed → reviewer removal, possible re‑review.
13. Electronic Submission Portals – Overview & Selection
13.1 NIH ASSIST
  • Web‑based in‑house tool for preparation, submission, and tracking; integrates with eRA Commons.
13.2 Institutional System‑to‑System (S2S)
  • Institution’s proprietary portal that packages the application and forwards to Grants.gov.
13.3 Grants.gov Workspace
  • Central NIH‑managed online system; handles form completion, validation, and submission.
13.4 Downloadable Forms/Package
  • Offline PDFs for manual assembly; submission triggers email receipts (submission, validation, tracking number).

Choose portal based on institutional availability; all routes obey identical registration & data requirements.

14. Post‑Award Management Essentials
  • After Notice of Award, upload signed NoA to eRA Commons.
  • Set up grant account in institution’s financial system; link to eRA Commons ID.
  • Comply with annual progress reports (RPPR) and financial statements via eRA Commons.
  • Monitor NIH policy updates (e.g., data sharing, human subjects protection) for ongoing compliance.
