Validation of Functional Lumen Imaging Probe Panometry Esophageal Motility Classification Version 2.0: A Study of 805 Patients
  • Journal: Neurogastroenterology & Motility
  • Publication Date: 22 October 2025
  • Funding: R01 DK137775 (J.E.P. & D.A.C.) from Public Health Service
  • Study Type: Multicenter validation study
Knowledge Test
  • 1. What is the negative predictive value of normal FLIP panometry v2.0 for disorders of EGJ outflow?
  • 2. What FLIP v2.0 classification is most strongly associated with Type I or II achalasia?
  • 3. What pressure threshold at 60 mL fill volume differentiates 'disordered' from 'diminished' contractile response in FLIP v2.0?
  • 4. What percentage of patients with non-spastic obstruction on FLIP v2.0 had a conclusive disorder of EGJ outflow on HRM/CCv4.0?
  • 5. What percentage of Type III achalasia patients on HRM were classified as spastic obstruction on FLIP panometry v2.0?
FLIP Panometry Overview
  • Assesses esophageal motility during sedated endoscopy;
  • Displays esophageal diameter topography + distensive pressure;
  • Evaluates EGJ opening mechanics + contractile response (CR) to luminal distension.
Advantages over HRM
  • Performed during sedated endoscopy → ↑ patient tolerance;
  • Real-time motility interpretation;
  • Procedure + interpretation ≈ several minutes.
FLIP v1.0 to v2.0 Refinements
  • Dallas Consensus introduced v2.0 → standardized protocol + refined classification;
  • EGJ opening criteria unchanged (nomenclature only);
  • CR patterns updated: impaired/disordered CR in v1.0 subdivided into diminished vs. disordered CR;
  • Obstruction w/ weak CR reclassified into non-spastic + spastic subtypes → improved achalasia phenotyping.
Key Changes in CR Patterns (v1.0 → v2.0)
  • Impaired/Disordered CR (v1.0) → Diminished CR OR Disordered CR (v2.0);
  • Differentiation based on 40 mmHg pressure threshold @ 60 mL fill volume.
Key Changes in Motility Classifications (v1.0 → v2.0)
  • Obstruction w/ weak CR (v1.0) → Non-spastic obstruction OR Spastic obstruction (v2.0);
  • Weak (v1.0) → Hypocontractility OR Possible spasm (v2.0);
  • Spastic reactive (v1.0) → Spastic obstruction OR Possible obstruction OR Possible spasm (v2.0).
Methodology
  • Multicenter study @ 2 tertiary esophageal centers;
  • 805 adult patients (18-91 yrs);
  • Consecutively enrolled in registries (Jan 2020 – May 2024);
  • Included: FLIP + HRM completed;
  • Excluded: technically limited studies, prior foregut surgery, esophageal mechanical obstruction (>3cm hiatal hernia, stricture, EoE, severe reflux esophagitis).
FLIP Study Protocol
  • 16-cm FLIP catheter (EndoFLIP EF-322N);
  • Performed during sedated EGD (moderate sedation/propofol);
  • Catheter positioned w/ distal 1-3 impedance sensors beyond EGJ;
  • Bag filled stepwise 10-mL increments (40 mL → 70 mL);
  • Each distension volume maintained 30-60s.
FLIP Data Analysis
  • Post-endoscopy using customized program (wklytics.com/nmgi);
  • Raters blinded to clinical details (HRM, TBE);
  • Interpretation used FLIP v1.0 + v2.0 criteria;
  • Assessed CR pattern (50-70 mL fill) + EGJ opening (EGJ-DI @ 60 mL, max EGJ diameter @ 60-70 mL).
HRM Protocol
  • 4.2-mm solid-state assembly;
  • Transnasal placement (hypopharynx to stomach);
  • CCv4.0 protocol: 10x 5-mL liquid swallows (supine), then 5x 5-mL liquid swallows (upright);
  • Investigators blinded to FLIP/TBE results.
HRM Diagnostic Criteria (CCv4.0)
  • Abnormal IRP: >15 mmHg (supine) OR >12 mmHg (upright).
Role of Timed Barium Esophagram (TBE)
  • Applied for inconclusive HRM (EGJOO, absent contractility w/ IRP @ upper limit of normal);
  • Upright position, 200 mL low-density barium;
  • Images @ 1, 2, 5 min;
  • 12.5 mm barium tablet if no significant retention @ 5 min.
TBE Abnormal Criteria
  • Barium column height >5 cm @ 5 min OR Barium column height >5 cm @ 1 min + tablet impaction → defines conclusive disorders of EGJ outflow.
TBE Normal Criteria
  • Indicates normal EGJ outflow.
TBE Inconclusive Criteria
  • Barium column height >5 cm @ 1 min & ≤5 cm @ 5 min (no tablet/tablet passage) OR Barium height ≤5 cm @ 1 min w/ tablet impaction → interpreted as inconclusive EGJ outflow.
  • Note: Inconclusive HRM/CCv4.0 results excluded from primary analysis (n=101); assessed in secondary analysis.
FLIP Panometry v2.0 Classification Criteria
  • Standardized definitions for contractile response (CR) patterns + EGJ opening classifications;
  • Used to assign overall motility classification.
Contractile Response (CR) Patterns
  • Evaluated during 50-70 mL fill volume.
Normal CR
  • >1 distinct antegrade contractions (≥6-cm axial length);
  • Associated w/ FLIP pressure rise >10 mmHg;
  • W/ or w/o repetitive antegrade contraction;
  • Not meeting spastic CR criteria.
Diminished CR
  • Contractility present, but not normal/spastic;
  • FLIP pressure (w/ 60 mL EGJ-DI) ≤40 mmHg.
Disordered CR
  • Contractility present, but not normal/spastic;
  • FLIP pressure (w/ 60 mL EGJ-DI) >40 mmHg.
Absent CR
  • No contractile activity in esophageal body.
Spastic CR
  • Presence of any: Sustained occluding contractions OR Sustained lower esophageal sphincter contractions.
EGJ Opening Classifications
  • Based on EGJ-DI @ 60 mL fill volume + maximum EGJ diameter @ 60-70 mL fill volume.
Reduced EGJ Opening
  • EGJ-DI <2.0 mm²/mmHg AND Max EGJ diameter <12 mm.
Inconclusive EGJ Opening
  • EGJ-DI <2.0 mm²/mmHg OR Max EGJ diameter <16 mm;
  • Not meeting 'reduced EGJ opening' criteria.
Normal EGJ Opening
  • EGJ-DI ≥2.0 mm²/mmHg AND Max EGJ diameter ≥16 mm.
FLIP Panometry v2.0 Motility Classifications
  • Combinations of EGJ opening + CR patterns.
Normal Motility
  • Normal EGJ opening + Normal CR.
Hypocontractility
  • Normal EGJ opening + Absent OR Diminished CR.
Possible Spasm
  • Normal EGJ opening + Spastic OR Disordered CR.
Possible Obstruction
  • Inconclusive EGJ opening + Anya CR pattern (CR pattern should be specified).
Spastic Obstruction
  • Reduced EGJ opening + Spastic OR Disordered CR.
Obstruction w/ Normal Contractility
  • Reduced EGJ opening + Normal CR.
Non-spastic Obstruction
  • Reduced EGJ opening + Absent OR Diminished CR.
Study Results
  • Primary analysis: 704 patients w/ conclusive HRM/CCv4.0 diagnoses;
  • Secondary analysis: 101 patients w/ inconclusive HRM/CCv4.0 diagnoses.
Cohort Characteristics (n=805)
  • Mean age: 55 (SD 17) yrs;
  • 62% female;
  • Dysphagia: 75% (most common indication);
  • 66% underwent FLIP + HRM on same day;
  • Most frequent HRM: Normal (33%), Achalasia (30%).
FLIP v2.0 Classification Distribution (Total Cohort)
  • Normal motility: 19%;
  • Hypocontractility: 11%;
  • Non-spastic obstruction: 23%;
  • Spastic obstruction: 11%;
  • Possible spasm: 7.2%;
  • Possible obstruction: 28%;
  • Obstruction w/ normal contractility: 0 patients.
Clinical Associations by FLIP v2.0 Classification
  • Dysphagia: common across all classifications;
  • Reflux: ↑ in normal, hypocontractility, possible spasm;
  • Mild esophagitis (LA A/B): ↑ in normal, hypocontractility;
  • Hiatal hernia (≤3cm): ↓ in non-spastic obstruction.
FLIP Panometry Evaluation Findings (Primary Analysis, n=704)
  • EGJ opening classifications:
  • Normal EGJ opening: 266 (38%) patients;
  • Inconclusive EGJ opening: 199 (28%) patients;
  • Reduced EGJ opening: 239 (34%) patients.
EGJ Opening & HRM Correlation
  • Normal EGJ opening: 98.5% had normal EGJ outflow per HRM/CCv4.0;
  • Reduced EGJ opening: 81.6% had disorder of EGJ outflow.
CR Patterns Distribution
  • Normal CR: 158 (22.4%) patients;
  • Diminished CR: 85 (12.1%) patients;
  • Absent CR: 252 (35.8%) patients;
  • Disordered CR: 113 (16.1%) patients;
  • Spastic CR: 96 (13.6%) patients.
CR & Achalasia/EGJOO
  • Only 1 patient w/ normal CR had achalasia (Type III);
  • Only 3 patients w/ normal CR had conclusive EGJOO.
FLIP v2.0 Motility Classifications & HRM/CCv4.0 Alignment
  • FLIP v2.0 classifications frequently paralleled HRM/CCv4.0 diagnoses.
Normal Motility (FLIP v2.0)
  • 137 patients classified as normal;
  • 81% had normal motility (HRM/CCv4.0);
  • 12% had IEM (HRM/CCv4.0);
  • 99% negative predictive value for disorders of EGJ outflow;
  • 0 patients w/ achalasia on HRM.
Hypocontractility (FLIP v2.0)
  • 78 patients classified as hypocontractility;
  • 72% had absent contractility or IEM (HRM/CCv4.0);
  • 27% had normal motility (HRM/CCv4.0);
  • 1 patient had Type II achalasia (treatment deferred due to minimal symptoms, normal TBE).
Non-spastic Obstruction (FLIP v2.0)
  • 163 patients classified as non-spastic obstruction;
  • 77% had Type I (31%) or Type II (47%) achalasia;
  • 8.0% had Type III achalasia;
  • 5.5% had conclusive EGJOO;
  • Total 91% had a disorder of EGJ outflow by HRM/CCv4.0.
Spastic Obstruction (FLIP v2.0)
  • 76 patients classified as spastic obstruction;
  • 32% had Type I (2.6%) or Type II (29%) achalasia;
  • 21% had Type III achalasia.
Type III Achalasia (HRM) & FLIP v2.0
  • Among 37 patients w/ Type III achalasia (HRM):
  • 43% classified as spastic obstruction (FLIP v2.0);
  • 35% classified as non-spastic obstruction (FLIP v2.0);
  • 22% classified as possible obstruction (FLIP v2.0).
Possible Obstruction (FLIP v2.0)
  • 199 patients classified as possible obstruction;
  • 38% had normal motility (HRM/CCv4.0);
  • 26% had Type I/II achalasia (HRM/CCv4.0);
  • 16% had IEM (HRM/CCv4.0).
Comparison: FLIP v1.0 vs. FLIP v2.0
  • Refinements in v2.0 aimed to improve alignment w/ HRM findings.
Obstruction w/ Weak CR (FLIP v1.0) → v2.0 Reclassification
  • v1.0: 26% Type I achalasia, 43% Type II achalasia, 10% Type III achalasia;
  • v2.0 Non-spastic obstruction: 31% Type I, 47% Type II, 8.0% Type III achalasia;
  • v2.0 Spastic obstruction: 3% Type I, 29% Type II, 21% Type III achalasia.
Weak (FLIP v1.0) → v2.0 Reclassification
  • v1.0: 32% absent contractility, 27% IEM, 2.7% hypercontractile esophagus;
  • v2.0 Hypocontractility: 44% absent contractility, 28% IEM, 0% hypercontractile/spasm;
  • v2.0 Possible spasm: 2.0% absent contractility, 24% IEM, 7.8% hypercontractile esophagus, 0% spasm.
Spastic Reactive (FLIP v1.0) → v2.0 Reclassification
  • v1.0: 13% Type III achalasia;
  • v2.0 Spastic obstruction: 21% of Type III achalasia;
  • v2.0 Possible obstruction: 4.0% of Type III achalasia;
  • v2.0 Possible spasm: 0% Type III achalasia.
Discussion & Clinical Implications
  • FLIP panometry v2.0 effectively categorizes esophageal motility;
  • Frequently aligns w/ HRM/CCv4.0 diagnoses;
  • Well-tolerated approach, complements/alternative to HRM.
Normal Motility (FLIP v2.0) Significance
  • High negative predictive value (99%) for disorders of EGJ outflow;
  • 0 patients w/ achalasia;
  • Can rule out achalasia → guide management towards reflux/functional syndrome w/o HRM;
  • Proportion of DES (1-14%) + IEM (7-17%) observed aligns w/ healthy volunteers.
Non-spastic Obstruction (FLIP v2.0) Significance
  • Strongly associated w/ achalasia or conclusive EGJOO;
  • Positive predictive value: 91%;
  • 77% were non-spastic (Type I/II) achalasia (vs. 69% w/ v1.0 obstruction w/ weak CR).
Refinement of Impaired/Disordered CR (IDCR) in v2.0
  • Differentiation into diminished CR vs. disordered CR (based on 40 mmHg pressure @ 60 mL EGJ-DI);
  • Improved subtyping of achalasia (non-spastic vs. spastic);
  • Spastic obstruction (v2.0) encompassed nearly half of Type III achalasia (16 patients, 21% of spastic obstruction cases);
  • Also included 29% Type II achalasia → suggests spastic variant of Type II achalasia identifiable by FLIP.
Hypocontractility (FLIP v2.0) Significance
  • Enhanced identification of hypocontractile disorders;
  • 72% had IEM or absent contractility (vs. 59% w/ v1.0 weak motility).
Possible Spasm (FLIP v2.0) Limitations
  • Intended to distinguish primary esophageal spasm from hypocontractile disorders;
  • Only 8% had hypercontractile esophagus or DES per HRM/CCv4.0;
  • 65% had normal HRM → suggests impaired secondary peristalsis, not spastic variant w/ normal HRM;
  • Area requires further refinement + validation.
Study Strengths
  • Large patient cohort (n=805) w/ comprehensive esophageal evaluations;
  • Application of HRM + CCv4.0;
  • Cohort distinct from initial FLIP v1.0 study;
  • Multicenter design → ↑ generalizability;
  • Higher prevalence of achalasia → strength, as achalasia identification is critical.
Study Limitations
  • Absence of definitive gold standard for diagnosis (key challenge in motility research);
  • Inconclusive/discordant diagnoses still occur w/ HRM + CCv4.0 + TBE;
  • FLIP goal not to replicate HRM (assess distinct physiological processes: FLIP secondary peristalsis, HRM primary peristalsis);
  • Two esophageal referral centers → may not reflect community practice patient characteristics.
Future Directions
  • Explore FLIP v2.0 application in broader populations;
  • Assess longitudinal outcomes;
  • Further define unique subgroups of interest;
  • Diagnostic paradigm will continue to develop w/ clinical experience + research.
